To the Latin American region include Mexico, Central America (Central American Isthmus and West Indies) and countries of the continent of South America surrounding islands. Among the countries in the region is a colony the United States, France, Great Britain and the Netherlands.

The name comes from the Latin America Latin underlying Romance languages: Spanish, Portuguese, French, where the majority of the population speaks the region.

Latin America, except for Cuba, within the group of developing countries, but in terms of economic development ahead of most of them developing countries, Africa and Asia. However, the levels of development of individual countries in the region, there are significant differences. Most developed in the region are Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Chile, Venezuela and Colombia. Their economy is a significant proportion of new manufacturing industries are machine building, metalworking, chemical and oil refining. Brazil, Mexico and Argentina belong to Nick. These countries are sometimes called big three. They account sq. / 3 industry production. Most developed countries in the region together in Mercosur (international card organizations). In Latin America, based on economic-integration formation of MERCOSUR, formed the fourth power center of world trade and finance.

Brazil - one of the largest in size of territory and rich natural resources of the world. Brazil wood stock reaches a third of the world. Opened in 1500 Brazil got its name from the chief at the time of natural wealth, which revealed the Portuguese colonialists in the country. It was a sandalwood tree (pau-brasil), which produced valuable red-violet.

Today, the country has large reserves of fresh water, hydropower and various minerals. Unlike most developing countries, in Brazil's industrial structure is dominated by manufacturing industries: metallurgy (seventh in the world in the production of steel), machinery (especially transport, aircraft, shipbuilding), and manufacturing of electronics and computers. An important source of income for the country is the production and export of coffee, which has become a national symbol of Brazil. In countries account for 1/4 of world production of instant coffee.

Less developed countries in the region is the Central American Isthmus and Bolivia. Small islands Caribbean specializing mainly in primary processing of agricultural raw materials (sugar, coffee, juice, canned food), or extraction of minerals (Jamaica) and the provision of travel services.

In countries with underdeveloped economic structure (Paraguay, Haiti) is dominated by artisanal enterprises manufacture of semi-finished products and consumer nature. In the Caribbean, is Cuba - a country centrally controlled economy. In MHPP it specializes in growing sugar cane, citrus, tobacco. The country has significant recreational resources.

On the South American continent are two sub-regions: the Andean (mountain country west of Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Chile) and La Plata or the Atlantic (countries of eastern mainland Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, Brazil). Differences geographical position of the two sub-regions are shown in the agricultural specialization of countries.

The modern economy of Latin America has some similarities historically and continue to drive the economic face of the region. This is, firstly, stratification sector (public sector, cooperative farming, handicraft production, estates - large private landholdings). Second, the narrow specialization of the economy: monokulturnist in agriculture monoproduktovist in the industry. Third, dependence on foreign capital and foreign debt problem.
