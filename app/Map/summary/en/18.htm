The collapse of the Russian and Austro-Hungarian empires that occurred as a result of World War I, helped the Rebirth of Ukraine. 1917 Ukrainian party and civil society organizations formed the Ukrainian Central Rada. It was led by Michael Grushevskii. In November 1917 it was proclaimed the Ukrainian National Republic (UNR). In April 1918, power passed to the Hetman Pavlo Soon-padskoho, and the official name of the state was the Ukrainian State. After the abdication of Hetman Ukraine government had to simultaneously fight a war against the Russian Bolsheviks, Whites and Poland. In these hard times power was UNR Directory. In Western Ukraine in November 1918 was proclaimed the Republic of the West, and January 22, 1919, both Ukrainian states united. However, the war was lost, and the neighboring states seized the territory of Ukraine.
