Recreational (Latin - recovery) resources - natural and man-made objects and phenomena that can be used for recreation, tourism and treatment.

Recreational resources are divided into: natural and recreational, cultural and historical.

Natural and recreational resources:

  • mountain landscapes suitable for relaxation, recreation and ski tourism;
  • coastal areas, river banks and lakes suitable for coastal and beach areas;
  • area with comfortable climate (warm islands of the seas and oceans), forest, mineral springs, mud, national parks, nature reserves or undeveloped exotic territory.

By cultural istorychnyhvidnosyat cultural sites, historical and architectural monuments, religious, religious centers, historic centers and more.

All objects recreation - whether architectural monument, or a picturesque landscape or nature reserve - constantly exposed to damaging factors: pollution, erosion, moisture, irresponsible human action, increasing urbanization, mass tourism. To preserve for future generations of the cultural and natural heritage of humanity, UNESCO adopted in 1972 the Convention on the Protection of the World Cultural and Natural Heritage. The Convention was a committee that approved the List of World Heritage sites, which is constantly updated. Making the List object attracts the attention of government and public organizations to the problems of preserving national heritage.

Availability and use of recreational resources play an important role in the economic development of many countries. In particular, Europe has a unique combination of natural and recreational, cultural and historical resources along with a developed transport infrastructure and excellent service attract the highest number of international tourists every year. Significant recreational resources are in Asia, but political instability in many regions of the world that hinders their full use. Great opportunity in the development of educational and sports tourism with countries in Africa, but due to low economic growth (primarily due to underdeveloped transport and services) they are used very little.

Rapidly developing oceanic tourism. The vast expanses of the oceans and seas provide excellent conditions for cruise tourism. Travel on comfortable sea liners enable wealthy tourists to get acquainted with exotic countries, visiting the most remote corners of the planet.
