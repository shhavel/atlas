In May 1648 launched the National Liberation War of the Ukrainian people against Polish domination. It was led by Bohdan Khmelnytsky. As a result of the first battles were released significant part of Ukrainian territory. These areas established Ukrainian Cossack state - Zaporozhian Host. As the highest authority in the state belonged to the hetman, historians call it even Hetmanate. The territory of the Zaporozhian Host was divided into regions, called host.

Looking for allies against Poland in 1654, B. Khmelnytsky was forced to seek help from the Tsar. This then led to the full integration of Ukraine into the Muscovite.
