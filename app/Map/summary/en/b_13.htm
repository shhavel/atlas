America in the XVIII century.

After the discovery of America by Columbus Spanish conquered the southern part of North America. The rest of the spaces inhabited continents free Indian tribes. These relatively harsh edges are not attracted to the Spaniards. Meanwhile, in the north Atlantic coast of America drew attention England, developed rapidly.

The beginning of the British colonization of North America began in May 1607 at the time of its coast at the mouth of the river unknown to Europeans landed 120 settlers who were sent to the London trading company. A year earlier, she gave away the rights to this territory King James I (in Engl. - James). In his honor, the settlers named the river James, and built a fort - Jamestown. The first British colony on American soil was named Virginia in honor of Queen Elizabeth I. The time between this event and the declaration of independence of US Americans call the colonial period.

Life of the first settlers in unfamiliar places was more severe than it seemed from distant Europe. Disease, hunger, clashes with local tribes led to heavy losses among the colonists. Yet the colonization of North America continued. A hundred years later the number of settlers increased to 300 thousand., And another 50 years to 1.2 million. After the independence of the US population is about 3.9 million. Pers. Over time, new settlements appeared on the north and south, along the coast of Spanish Florida to New England (13 colonies). Each of them was created independently with its own access to the sea, with their orders.

So New England was founded by a group of Puritans who fled religious persecution (102 Pilgrim father), who landed on the shores of Cape strict Code from the ship "Mayflower" ("May Flower") in 1620 before going ashore, signed an agreement on joint construction of life according to the laws and customs of the faith and morals of the Puritans. Subsequently, at the landing site was founded by the city of New Plymouth. This Day in US celebrated as a holiday - the Day of the Pilgrim Fathers or traveler-worshipers. Puritan settlers had to endure a tough year when they oblashtovuvaly life. Considerable assistance they had the local Indians. Grateful colonists staged in 1621 in Plymouth lunch, inviting the Indians. This event marked the beginning of the holiday - Thanksgiving.

Being mostly subjects of the English crown, the colonists felt freer than in England itself. Here, the new lands did not exist the traditions and customs that oplutuvaly life of Europeans. Any achievements of the colonists were the result of their hard work, so they quickly realized their freedom and independence. In solving the problems they preferred self-organization, and not the search of a patron. This situation quickly led to the democratization of all aspects of life in the colonies. All this became the basis of a peculiar nature of future Americans. Courage, energy, enterprise, and democracy were the main features of the new American nation.

British authorities felt that her colonies in the New World are becoming more rebellious. By the middle of the XVII century. colonists themselves provide almost everything you need. Gradually began to grow in colonies patriotic movement, which eventually turned into a struggle for independence from England. At the root of this movement was a distinguished philosopher and educator, scholar and writer, politician and diplomat Benjamin Franklin (1706-1790 gg.)

In the middle of the XVIII century. relations between Britain and its colonies worsened. Ban on new lands in the West and the new taxes caused a storm of discontent of the colonists. They are increasingly began to demand representation in the British Parliament: "No taxation without representation". Finally the confrontation between the colonies and England into the open and led to war. Striking episode confrontation was "Boston Tea Party" when the sea was thrown cargo of tea arrived from England.

In September-October 1774 representatives of 56 colonies gathered in Philadelphia on I Continental Congress. He accepted the Declaration of the Rights and called for a boycott of British goods.

April 19, 1775 near the town of Lexington and Concord occurred first armed clashes between militias of colonists and British troops. This day is considered the beginning of the war for independence. The first victories instilled confidence in the colonists. In May 1775, the Continental Congress met AI, one of the first decisions was to create a regular army, it was headed by George Washington (1732-1799 gg.)

At the suggestion of the Congress of the colony began to form their own government, and some even declared their independence. July 4, 1776, Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence. Its author was Thomas Jefferson (1743-1826 biennium) and John Adams (1735-1826 gg.) Declaration proclaimed the establishment of a new independent state - the United States of America (USA). Meanwhile, British troops captured Philadelphia (the first capital of the United States), New York. Washington's army could hardly contain the enemy. It becomes clear that the expense of only one of enthusiasm not win the war. Then in September 1776, Congress adopted a decision on the conclusion of the war to distribute the land to its members for the Appalachian mountains. Hope this will strengthen the morale of the army, increased the flow of volunteers. To help the Americans arrived and volunteers from Europe: French Marquis Lafayette outfitted a ship, the German Baron Wilhelm Shteyben civil colonists created a real army, Pole Tadeusz Kosciuszko. Finally there was a crisis in the war. The first significant victory of the American army was near the town of Saratoga (October 1777). After this, a new state admitted France, Spain, Holland. In victory, the American army launched a decisive offensive in Yorktown and surrounded and forced kapitulyuvity main forces of the British (October 19 1781).

September 3, 1783 between Britain and the United States signed a peace treaty in Versailles (France). Britain recognized the independence of the United States and give them the territory from the Atlantic coast to the Mississippi River.

The final design of the US constitutional held September 17, 1787, when the Constitution was adopted, with some amendments, which applies to the present. The first US president, George Washington was elected.

The first 10 amendments were formulated by George Madison and decorated in the Bill of Rights (1791).

Over time, was adopted and national symbols.

June 14, 1777, Congress approved the national flag of 13 red and white horizontal stripes and in the corner on a blue background 13 stars, symbolizing the first 13 states of the state. Subsequently, the number of stars increases as the number of states. Today, there are 50. In 1782 was approved by the Coat of Arms, and in 1931 the state anthem.
