Countries in the region are very different in size, political system, level of economic development. China - a country with one-party political system and centrally controlled economy that has some of the world's highest rates of annual growth in GNP. North Korea (DPRK) - isolated state with hypertrophied toward the military-industrial complex economy and great food problems. South Korea, along with Taiwan and other countries in the Asia-Pacific region refer to NEC Asia.

China - the third and the first area in population of the world. The country is distinguished by the diversity of the natural environment and wealth of natural resources. The ancient Chinese civilization left behind a lot of attractions.

Modern China is an industrial-agrarian country, which along with the traditional industries (textiles, coal, metallurgy) have developed new industries: Aerospace and nuclear industry, mechanical engineering, electronics and petrochemicals. Since '80 the twentieth century. China's economy is undergoing a period of fundamental reform. In the state's economy heavily involved foreign capital, established special economic zones, which give 10% of exports. Dynamic economies of these regions are the engines of the entire economy of the state. The growth rate of industrial and agricultural products is so high that the production of many products China among the top ten world leaders. China has collected a third world production of rice and millet crops. Country - one of the world's largest producers of corn, cotton, tea, soybeans, tobacco, poultry, silkworm cocoons. Textile industry in terms of production of finished products ranked first in the world. China came in second in the world in the production of steel. And although the production output per capita, China still lags far behind developed countries, in terms of GDP the country came in second place in the world, second only to the USA. However, the territorial structure of China has seen significant disparities: 80% industry and most of the agricultural production accounts for East China. In the western part of the country only developed mining industry and extensive livestock.

The economy, the pace of economic development of the two countries differ considerably. North Korea - closed industrial-agrarian state with a centrally controlled economy type. South Korea in the early 60s of the last century was typical of a backward country. With limited natural resources, underdeveloped economy, the country began to develop industries that rely on the country's main resource - cheap labor. Products for labor intensive shoemaking, textile, woodworking industries was oriented towards export. Removing all barriers to trade and foreign investment has led to rapid capital accumulation, efficient allocation of resources and access to modern technological level of the economy. Now South Korea occupies the leading position in the manufacture of high technology products, consumer electronics, computers, microelectronics, automobiles. Became widely known brands of household appliances (Samsung, LG, Daewoo) and cars (Daewoo, Hyundai, Kia) produced in this country. The structure of the country's exports are 95% finished product.

Mongolia belongs to the post-socialist transition economies. The basis of the economy - agriculture, which employs 40% of the working population. Production of the main branch of agriculture - livestock is the raw material for light and food industry and is part of the internal trade turnover of the country and more than 1/3 of the value of exports. Is also developed mining industry. It found significant deposits of coal and lignite, uranium, gold, silver, copper, tin, iron ore, phosphates.
