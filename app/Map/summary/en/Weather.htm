Due to the rotation of the Earth around the Sun is a change of seasons. The usual winter, spring, summer, autumn last three calendar months. However, the onset and duration of the seasons, the natural calendar different from the calendar chronology usual. This means that given the key indicators weather (temperature, precipitation, wind, humidity) can be determined when actually starts and how long each season.

Thus, the fall in the city actually begins 10-12 September, when average daily temperatures fall below 15 °. Dry and warm at the beginning, it brings us abundant rains in November.

At the end of November accounted for the beginning of winter, at this time the average daily temperatures become negative and snow falls. Although winter in Kiev, as a rule, not very cold, it lasts long enough (up to 120 days). A frequent thaws with piercing winds create many problems for Kiev.

From late March to late May in Kiev continues spring. It was in late March, the land is mainly released from snow, and the average daily temperature exceeds 0 °. Kyiv is especially beautiful in May, when it is warm enough but not hot, and green lace plant has not yet felt the hot breath of summer. At the end of May, when the average temperature is higher than 15 ° C in our city come summer. This time the weather is characterized by relatively high temperatures and frequent downpours with thunderstorms (especially in early summer). It was during the warmer months in Kiev for a large part of the annual precipitation. However, often occur in drought, when there is no rain for several weeks. Then the wonderful Kyiv parks and gardens lose their unique beauty and Kiev beaches and recreation areas are filled with thousands of tourists.

Constant observation of the weather leading to the meteorological stations. Using special instrumentation specialists, meteorologists record air temperature and soil, atmospheric pressure, wind strength and direction, rainfall. All weather data for many years and used to keep weather forecasting. These forecasts are used in many spheres of life, including transport, agriculture, home, during mass public events.
