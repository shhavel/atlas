Sovereign countries have different forms of state - the state system, which consists of government and form administrative-territorial structure.

There are two forms of government: monarchy and republic. Republic - is a form of government in which the supreme legislative power is vested in Parliament (nationwide representative elected body). Depending on how the divided powers between parliament and the president of the republic may be presidential, parliamentary or a presidential-parliamentary form of government.

Monarchy (Greek monarchia - undivided) - is a form of government in which the supreme power is formally wholly or partly owned by the sovereign (king, prince, sultan, emir, emperor, etc.) and is inherited. Monarchy may be absolute, when the power of the monarch actually unlimited (Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Brunei) and constitutional (UK, Spain, Japan, Morocco), where the monarch's power is limited by the constitution, legislative functions were transferred to the parliament, the executive - the government and the monarch which is formally the supreme power carrier actually performs representative functions.

A variation of absolute monarchy is a theocratic monarchy, when the monarch - the head of the Church (Vatican, Saudi Arabia).

Especially in the world and the only form of government is the Jamahiriya (Libya), which in Arabic - the country of the masses. The official name of the country - the Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya.

After the Second World War and the collapse of the British Empire in the Commonwealth remained the most former British possessions.
