General Information

It lies in the lower reaches of the Dnieper River in southern Ukraine, within the Black Sea Lowland, bordering the Black and Azov seas and the Bay Lagoon. Surface - undulating plain, tilted to the south. The northern part has a height of 50-90 m south - 10-50 m. The coast of the Azov Sea and the Gulf of Siwash high above sea level at 5.10 m. The surface of the southern part is mostly flat, broken podami (6-8 m depth) and balkamy- lengthy (depth - 10-15 m) and artificial mounds, tombs (20 meters high). On the left bank extends 140 km long strip of sand with dunes Oleshkivski (height 8-15 m). The coastline is rugged seas shallow bays and estuaries, which are separated by a small peninsula and long narrow sand spit.

The mineral resources most common limestone, shell, clay, sand, salt Sivash, brine, mud. There are peat, natural gas, bauxite. At intervals of 2-3 years in spring and autumn there are droughts and dry winds. Rivers longer than 10 km Only 6 Main River - River (lower reaches) with inflow Inhulets. Near the mouth of the Dnieper is divided into sleeves and forms Dnieper wetlands. In the valleys of the rivers are small ponds and lakes, on the Dnieper - Kakhovske reservoir. In the southern part a channel: Pivnichnokrymskyy, Kahovskij and Krasnoznam'yanskyy. Among the zonal soil types dominated Southern black humus and slabohumusni that vary southern black earth alkaline and south - colored saline soils. Natural steppes are preserved only in the river valleys and on the slopes of hills. The steppes are covered with fescue-grass grasses in floodplains and podah - meadows. Lower sand lined with pine trees, orchards, vineyards. On the sandy arenas - oak and birch nuts, xerophytic grasses. Historical Background

The first settlements in the region were present during the time of the late Paleolithic. In VII-III. e. e. Kherson steppes were part of the Scythian state. In the Middle Ages steppe is dominated by nomadic peoples, Pechenegs, Cumans, Tatars.

In the XVI century. These lands are beginning to fight and settle the Cossacks, who carried out numerous campaigns against the Turks and Tatars. In the 1709-1734 biennium. On the lower Dnieper Kamyanskiy there (near the village. Novokayiry) and then Oleshkivska (near the city. Tsyurupynsk) Host. Kherson city arose after the Russo-Turkish war in 1778 on the site of a small military fort. In 1783 the stocks of Kherson shipyard came the first frigate. In the nineteenth century. begins the agricultural colonization of Southern steppes. In addition to Kherson Ukrainian immigrants arriving from other parts of the Russian Empire and foreigners. In those days, most of the modern Kherson was a Taurian province with its center in Simferopol, Kherson province lying west of the Dnieper. In Soviet times, much of this land was included in the Mykolayiv region, and only in the postwar years created a modern Kherson region on land seized from Mykolaiv and Zaporizhia oblasts.
