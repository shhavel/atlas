Recreational (Latin - "recovery") resources - is natural and anthropogenic objects and phenomena that can be used for recreation, tourism and treatment.

Recreational resources are divided into natural and recreational, cultural and historical.

Natural and recreational resources:

- Mountain landscapes suitable for leisure, recreation and ski tourism;

- Seashores, rivers and lakes suitable for seaside and beach holidays;

- Areas with a comfortable climate (heat island seas and oceans), forests, mineral springs, mud, national parks, nature reserves or undeveloped exotic territory.

For cultural and historical sites include cultural, historical and architectural monuments, religious and religious complexes, historical centers, etc.

All recreation facilities - a landmark or scenic landscape or nature conservation area - constantly test the impact of destructive factors: pollution, erosion, waterlogging, the irresponsible actions of a person, increasing urbanization, mass tourism. To preserve for future generations of the cultural and natural heritage of humanity by UNESCO in 1972 adopted a Convention on the Protection of the World Cultural and Natural Heritage. Under the Convention, a committee was formed, which approved the List of World Heritage sites, this list is constantly updated. Adding to the List object attracts the attention of government and public organizations to the problems of preserving the national heritage.

Availability and use of recreational resources play an important role in the economic development of many countries. In particular, the European countries have a unique combination of natural and recreational, cultural and historical resources, which together with the development of transport infrastructure and good service each year attract many foreign tourists. Significant recreational resources are available to the countries of Asia, but political instability in many regions of the world that hinders their full use. Ample opportunities in the development of cognitive and sports tourism are African, but because of the low economic development (in particular this underdeveloped transport and services), they are used very little.

Rapidly developing oceanic tourism. Space oceans and seas provide excellent conditions for cruise tourism. Travelling in comfortable sea liners allow wealthy tourists to get acquainted with the exotic countries, visiting the most remote corners of the planet.
