Basic concepts and terms

Nobility - Ukrainian tribal nobility, aristocracy. Word Gentry denotes a group of people known and well-born. The nobility was the top of society and its elite. For many centuries served as the political leader of the Ukrainian nation, its exponent and defender of national interests. Representatives of this class of committed political leadership, put some political purpose, contributed to the development of culture and education, supported by the church and fed the people sense of national identity.

The magnates - the most powerful and influential landowners, mostly princes.

The shop was a self-governing community of free artisans of one or more specialties, headed by elected officers - tsehmistrom. In addition to artists, the shop were their assistants - apprentice (comrades) and students (condyles, guys). Each shop had its written charter, approved by the king or the owner of a private city. Statute defines as a particular production, and general lifestyle artisans.

1. The leading role of the nobility as top Ukrainian society in contemporary political life

After Krevsk Union, and especially after the defeat Švitrigaila many Ukrainian and Belarusian princes lost their fiefdoms.

There was a gradual convergence prince layers with other privileged classes and as a consequence the single privileged class - the gentry.

From the mid-15th century. in most Ukrainian lands established this structure dominant state: princes - gentlemen - zem'yany-Gentry - Gentry-rabble - armored nobles.

This structure continued until the mid-17th century.

However even completely impoverished princes were more likely to receive high government compared to less noble, even and wealthy nobleman.

In addition, the rulers acted in public service to the Lithuanian, and Polish nations, while maintaining their principality and receiving from the state to private ownership and lifelong holding large land holdings.

So princely families of Ostrog, Vyshnevetsky Koretsky, Czartoryski family, Zbarazkiy held a prominent government positions in the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, sat on the board of the Grand Duke, and in military campaigns were under their own banners.

Loss of Ukrainian state-princes role

With the increasing influence of the Polish rulers lost their ability at the national level to decide the fate of their homeland - she increasingly began to depend on the will of foreign capitals: free or Krakow.

However princely families still had power and authority to do local governance. Dividing each other first Volhynia and later Central Ukraine, they have assumed real power on the ground.

Their possessions prince acted right with their own taxes and seizures. It was his army.

So locally, seemed to have remained the same as for the Middle Ages.

Actually, as representatives of the princely families linked their genealogies of the princes of Kievan Rus.

In contemporary Ukraine occupied a special place family Ostrog.

2. Ukrainian Church and the state of the clergy

There is still a special place in Ukrainian society occupied the clergy, which was a separate social groups.

According to researchers spiritual condition was fairly large, it belonged to almost a tenth of the population - priests with their families, monks, people who serve the church.

Insulation clergy manifested in the fact that it was not subject to secular court.

During the 14th - 16th century. the Orthodox church and the clergy were not the same.

In the 14th century. Orthodox, Ruthenian, faith was adopted by the Lithuanian princes.

The authority of the Orthodox Church, which was supported by most of the population Ukrainian lands remained quite high, however, the gradual spread of Catholicism among the princely court, the pressure from the Catholic state, which was Poland, became increasingly stronger.

After Krevsk Union of Orthodox Lithuanians and Lithuanian pagans were gathered to Catholicism.

Lithuanian Catholic boyars equalized the rights of the Polish gentry.

But the act that showed the division of society on religious grounds, and opened the way for the oppression of the Orthodox Union became Horodelska 1413, according to which participation in government and to hold government positions have the right to exclusively Catholic.

However, certain guarantees of their position in the Ukrainian Orthodox were aristocracy, whose members were constantly on the board of the Grand Duke of Lithuania and the highest post.

On the Ukrainian territory, which was distributed to Polish administrative and political structure of the Orthodox church was difficult.

A characteristic feature of policy powers of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania regarding the Orthodox Church was the desire to have within their state independent church organization and rid the church depends Muscovy.

In the mid-15th century. finally there was a division of the Kiev Metropolia of Kiev and Moscow.

Cathedral of the Moscow bishops without the approval of Constantinople put a single metropolitan of Kiev remained its metropolitan subordinate to the Patriarch of Constantinople.

And no matter how difficult was the position of the Orthodox Church, it has also become a defender and representative of Ukrainian and Belarusian people in the Catholic Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, the focus of Ukrainian and Belarusian culture and spirituality.

3. The position of Ukrainian peasants

The lowest point of the social pyramid that society sat the peasants who were the largest layer - up to 80% of the population.

As before, the villagers living communities.

Arable land divided between the parts of the community - yard, which brings together some "smoke" (courtyards), ie economies of individual families.

A variety of taxes to the state and feudal rural community also distributed among the yard.

The community elected its own judgment. He was called dig the "cop" - a collection of communities.

Gradually dig trial court was replaced by a very feudal lords (patrimonial) and community land now owned by feudal lords.

Historians conventionally divided villagers into "mortgage" (personally free) and "nepohozhyh" (attached to their inheritance).

In addition, the nature of duties among farmers distinguish, Danes, traction, servants.

Tributaries - the largest group of "mortgage" farmers - called those whose primary duty was to pay tribute in kind or money for the use of arable lands and lands.

Some farmers had to work in the household of his ruler with his "pulled" (draft animals and equipment), that serve serfdom.

A privileged position among the peasants had servants who engaged in agriculture, while serving military service in the border castles. They took part in military campaigns, carried out various assignments.

5. Social by peasants

Ukrainian peasants did not put up with increasing social exploitation and fought against it, often taking up arms.

The most important speech of this period was the uprising of Ukrainian and Moldovan farmers in Pokuttya and Northern Bukovina, part of Moldavia (1490-1492 gg.).

Researchers stress that it was not only anti-social but also liberating character, that was intended not only to improve the situation of the peasants as social class (reduction obligations), and pursued national liberation because farmers were from other people, and those in power - more.

In the ranks of the rebels, in addition to Ukrainian and Moldavian peasants, commoners were even representatives of the Ukrainian Orthodox nobility.

Campaigns against the Polish troops led by Mucha.

Year 1491 insurgency resumed. The case continued predecessor Andrei Bahrul. However, both peasant uprisings were suppressed.
