General Information

It lies in the central part of Ukraine, in the middle reaches of the Dnieper. By the relief region is divided into two parts - the highland right bank and left bank, low-lying. Right-bank part, to the significant development of erosional landforms, lies in the Dnieper hills (266 m). Along the Dnieper River valley between the villages Trahtemyrovom and hop band over 70 km long stretch Kanivski Mountains (80-253 m), and the right bank of river. Robin extends Moshnohirskyy ridge (160-180 m). The left bank of the Dnieper region rozlyahayetsya lowlands and is a flat, pohorbovanu, terraced plains (80-150 m), sometimes swampy. Among the non-metallic minerals predominate, including: lignite (brown coal basin of the Dnieper), peat, building materials (various granite, labradorite, sandstone, gneiss, quartz sand). More than 400 deposits of various clays including bentonite. There are deposits of kaolin, bauxite, graphite. Possible development of new deposits of phosphates, apatite deposits of titanium and gold. Left part of the region has considerable deposits of mineral waters identified sources of radon water. The climate is continental with mild winters (-5,9 ° in January) and warm, dry summers slightly (+ 20 ° in July). The annual precipitation is 450-520 mm. The region has 181 stream length of 10 km. The main water artery - Dnieper and its tributaries (Ross, Robin and Tyasmyn - right, Sula and Supy - left). Rivers and Mountain Tikich Rotten Tikich belong to the basin of the Southern Bug. In the region - 33 reservoirs, including part of Kanev and Kremenchug reservoirs, over 650 floodplain lakes and ponds. Soils - mostly typical black soil humus and slabohumusni under forests - light gray, gray forest, and dark gray podzol soils. Is alluvial sandy and sandy-loam soils (on the left bank of the river), and in swampy floodplains - peat. Forests cover 15% of the area. Pine forests, pine forests, oak-hornbeam groves forming forests in the valleys of the Dnieper and Tyasmyn. Oak-hornbeam forests occur among the cultivated areas in the watersheds and slopes of hills. Floodplain forests of alder, willow, poplar grow in the river valleys to wetland areas. The wildlife area is diverse, but a few. There are 41 species of mammals, 275 - birds, 39 - Fish, 11 - 8 amphibians and reptiles. Among the adverse natural processes - the plane wash and deep erosion, abrasion and sliding phenomena along the banks of reservoirs, watersheds and suffusion on loess terraces of the Left Bank.

Reclamation: creating ravine and gully erosion and water protection (along the river) afforestation, reforestation sands. Cherkasy region was a part of, where at the beginning of a new era formed the nucleus of early East Slavic tribes, and at the time of Kievan Rus was a principality of Kiev. In the 1239-1240 biennium. These lands were devastated by the Mongols. In 1384 appeared the first mention of m. Cherkasy, which since the end of the XV century. became one of the centers of the Ukrainian Cossacks.
