In the history of the Byzantine Empire, the last period of its power is considered day Comneni Dynasty (1081-1204 gg.). The emperors of this dynasty a century revived the power and glory of the empire. The founder of the dynasty, Alex and Trebizond managed, at a time when death seemed inevitable Byzantium, to save the country by defeating the Seljuk Turks, Normans and Pechenegs.

For these Comneni Byzantium continued to lead a successful war. The most dangerous enemy of the Byzantines remained Rumskyy Sultanate formed by the Seljuk Turks. In 1176 the Emperor Manuel Comnenus I (1143-1180 gg.) Has launched an ambitious campaign against the Seljuks, but in the battle of Asia Minor city Miriokefala uschent was defeated them. After that battle, the Byzantines lost most of their possessions in Asia Minor. At the end of the XII century. The Byzantine Empire in all its borders on the defensive.

In 1204 during the Fourth Crusade, the Crusaders conquered Constantinople and formed the Latin Empire. However, Byzantine lands to conquer all the Latins could not. Orthodox Byzantine successor states were: Empire of Nicaea, Despotate of Epirus, Empire of Trebizond. The organizer of the revival of the Byzantine Empire was the Empire of Nicaea.

Byzantine Empire was restored by the Nicene emperor Michael VIII Palaeologus (1259-1282 gg.), The founder of the last dynasty of Byzantine emperors. It happened in 1261, when his army was almost no resistance took Constantinople.
