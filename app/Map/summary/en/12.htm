General Information

It lies in the east of Ukraine in the basin of the Seversky Donets. The northern part of the region - polohohvylyasta Lesova plain (120-240 m spurs Serednoruskoyi hills, dissected by river valleys, ravines and gullies), the southern part - Wavy ridges, plateau-like Lesova plain, very dissected by river valleys and steep gorges (Donetsk hill - 200 250 m, the highest point of the entire Left-bank Ukraine - G. Grave-Mechetna, 367 m). The terrain is difficult domes, mane, ridges and man-made forms - heaps, quarries, mounds. The region is rich in coal, coke, dovhopolumenyste, gas (part of the Donetsk coal basin). There are deposits of natural gas, building materials, marl, chalk, fluxing limestone, sandstone, clay. Opening mineral springs. The climate is continental with dry, hot summers (21 °, + 22 ° in July) and cold dry winters (-7 °, -8 ° in January). Precipitation is 400-500 mm per year, 70% of the precipitation falls during the warmer months. In the area of ​​about 120 rivers longer than 6 100 km. The main river - the Seversky Donets (265 km in the region) and its tributaries: Aydar, Derkul, Red (left) Luhannyu, Great Kamianka (right). In the south flowing by. Mius tributaries. In the region around 60 lakes, 60 reservoirs, 298 ponds. Soils - mostly ordinary black (81% of the area), turf schebenyuvatymy erodibility of 50-80%, in the valley of the Seversky Donets - black, turf sandy soils (erodibility 54-64%). Forests cover 8.6% of the territory. Natural forest is small, most of them planted. There ravine forests (oak, ash, maple, elm, pear, apple), floodplain (alder, aspen, poplar, willow) and watershed (oak, ash, maple, pear). Area shelter belts 20.5 ths. Ha (acacia, oak, maple, etc.). The fauna is poor. There are about 250 species of birds, 60 - mammals, 9 - reptiles and 47 - fish. Modern negative natural processes: linear and planar erosion washout, landslides, wind erosion, salinization, siltation of rivers. In 80-90% of coal rapidly developing erosion and denudation. Reclamation activities: planting shelterbelts, erosion creating ponds, vodozatrymuvalnyh shafts zaterasovuvannya slopes, land reclamation. In the 124 territories and objects protected areas, including national importance: the nature reserves, natural monuments 2, 2 park monuments of landscape architecture and 15 natural reserves.

Historical Background

During the Copper and Bronze Age land of settled agricultural tribes, cattle breeding. We found numerous monuments of Scythian-Sarmatian time (VII cent. BC. E. - III c. BC. E.). From the X century. of Donetsk land was under the influence of Kievan Rus, but lived here, mostly nomadic tribes. In the XIII century. the lands captured by the Mongol-Tatars. At the end of the XV - beginning of XVI century began development of the Donetsk steppe Cossacks. More intensive settlement of the land began in the XVII century. The leading role is played by the Cossacks and the development of land north of the Seversky Donets River, which became known as Sloboda Ukraine. In the first quarter of XVIII century. this work began with the use of coal. In 1795 began the construction of the Lugansk foundry, the needs of which were mine coal in the Fox buyeratsi (today Lysychansk). In 1795 on the site of modern Lugansk laid cannon foundry, which was to ensure the needs of the Black Sea Fleet. When the plant was established settlement called "Village Lugansk plant." In 1882, together with the adjacent settlement Broad Stone joined them and turned the city of Lugansk. In June 1938 Voroshilovgrad region was established, and in 1990 it was renamed in Luhansk region.
