Ukrainian lands in the Austrian and Russian empires in the early nineteenth century.

Subdivisions in the first 50 years of the nineteenth century.

At the beginning of the nineteenth century., Because of the three partitions of Poland, the Russian-Turkish wars, eliminate Hetman of the Zaporozhian Sich Ukrainian lands were ruled by the Russian and Austrian empires.

Russia had Slobozhanshchina, Left, Right Bank and South, representing about 85% of the land inhabited Ukrainian. As part of the Austrian Empire were approximately 15% of Ukrainian lands, namely, Eastern Galicia, northern Bukovina and Transcarpathia. The territories that were under the Russian Empire, called the bank Ukraine, in the Austrian Empire - Western.

After joining the Ukrainian lands in the Russian Empire, they were distributed to general administrative structure. By the mid-nineteenth century. 10 Governor-General of three located in the Dnieper. General Government - large territorial-administrative entity composed of several provinces. Governor general appointed and dismissed only the emperor, he had almost unlimited power (military, administrative, judicial, financial). The structure of the three governor-generalship on Ukrainian lands were 9 provinces in which administrative and executive function exercised by governors. Provinces divided into districts, which were headed by superintendent.

Subdivisions not take into account the ethnic composition of the population. On the Left Bank accounted for 95% of the Ukrainian population Slobozhanschine - 86% Right Bank - 85% South - 74%. Many Ukrainian inhabited Kuban, Don, of Voronezh, Kursk, Grodno, Mogylivsk and Bessarabia province.

The most numerous national minority in the Ukrainian lands were Jews. After the first partition of Poland, the Russian government introduced in 1791 for Jews "Pale of Settlement", beyond which the representatives of the minorities to settle forbidden. The ban lasted until 1917 All Dnieper, except Slobozhanshchina, included in the "Pale of Settlement". The event testified that the Russian government has a policy of discrimination on ethnic grounds. In addition, Jews were forbidden to settle in Kiev, Nikolaev, Sevastopol, government and Cossack villages of Poltava. Many cities have set up special Jewish quarters.

As part of the Austrian empire earth inhabited Ukrainian, belonged to different administrative units of the empire. Austrian authorities just do not pay attention to the ethnic composition of the population, when performed administrative and territorial division. Yes, shidnohalytski land (where a majority of Ukrainian) and zahidnohalytski land (where a majority of Poles) were one of the administrative-territorial unit called "Kingdom of Galicia and Ladomeriyi" with its capital in Lviv. As a representative body edges securely domination Polish nobility, though it was not the decisive force local governments. Completeness of all administrative power concentrated in the hands of the governor, then governor, appointed by the Emperor. "Kingdom" was divided into 18 counties (districts) of which 12 made up of Ukrainian land. Some districts to 1861 was part of Bukovina (including without ethnic division, although Northern Bukovina predominantly Ukrainian population and Southern - Romanian).

Transcarpathian Ukraine subordinated Pozhonskomu oversight and management of the Hungarian Kingdom was divided into four comitates. All power in comitates belonged to the administrators, who were appointed from among the large landowners. Most of the province's population were peasants-Rusyn enslaved Hungarian landowners.

The entire population of the Dnieper Ukraine was divided by social class basis of the nobility, clergy, burghers and peasants. The bulk of the population were peasants. The situation of farmers was different. 40-45% of farmers formed the serfs. There were also state farmers who were personally dependent, were able to maintain their own farm, paying a quarter of their income as tax to the state.

In Western Ukraine and peasants constituted the majority. Own national elite Ukrainian edge actually had, it was Polonized or rumunizovana. The only representative of the educated strata in eastern Galicia and Transcarpathia were Greek Catholic clergy, which also launched in this region national revival. In Bukovina resisted Romanization commit a layer of small amount of Orthodox priests.

Thus, at the turn of XVIII-XIX centuries. Ukrainian lands in the Russian and Austrian empires were in different conditions, which led to differences in their development. The leading idea, which in the nineteenth century. inspired by Ukrainian patriots, was the realization of belonging to a single nation, though torn in half and enslaved the two empires.
