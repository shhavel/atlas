Basic concepts and terms

"Russkaya Pravda" - a collection, or set, of Russian laws, which consisted for several generations. The oldest part is created at the time of Yaroslav the Wise; constitute a separate part of the article concluded Yaroslav sons - "Charter Yaroslavichy." By "Russian Truth" includes articles and later times. The collection was in force on the lands of Ukraine, Belarus, Lithuania, Russia for several centuries, became the basis for the development of law and justice.

Gospel (from the Greek. Good news) - one of the most important parts of the New Testament, consisting of the first four books written by the evangelists Matthew, Mark, Luke, John. It describes the earthly life of the Lord, the Son of God, Jesus Christ, His teachings, miracles, the cross of suffering, death, resurrection and ascension into heaven.

Graffiti - inscriptions and drawings scratched on the walls and architectural details of old buildings, vessels and other items that they found during archaeological excavations.

Mosaic - machinery decorating walls and floors, which cement based in a certain order, the small pieces of colored stone or glass - smalt.

Cathedral - the main or large church in the city; the main church of the monastery. Cathedral in pravoslvniy church called a great assembly of representatives of the clergy and laity to discuss important issues and are adopted binding regulations.

Patriarch - the highest title a priest in the Orthodox Church.

Metropolitan - the second rank after the patriarch. It stands at the head of the church-unit - Metropolis.

Deacon - assistant priest in the celebration of the church service, a lower spiritual rank.

Monk (chenets) - a believer who renounced ordinary secular (worldly) life for the benefit of serving God.

Monastery - the complex of buildings and structures intended for habitation of monks, the administration of religious rites, training and more.

Yaroslav the Wise was lucky, say researchers, is much more than all his predecessors and even many successors. He lived a long life and died at the height of her fame and power. 2. Establishment of Yaroslav Vladimirovich of Kiev

In the year 1019 the chronicler noted: "He sat Yaroslav in Kiev, wiped the sweat from his wife, showing the victory and great work."

Soon after the establishment of Kiev Yaroslav was forced to leave the capital and go to the northern lands to end the assault on Novgorod prince of Polotsk, Yaroslav nephew, Brjachislav.

Once a conflict has been resolved, as in force new - with Tmutarakansky prince Mstislav, who in 1023 accidentally took his wife to Kyiv. Mstislav accounted Jaroslav younger brother.

Previous intestine wars, he, according to the chronicler, did not participate. As for his claim to Kyiv, they got an unexpected development.

The fact that Mstislav won the battle of Yaroslav, but refused to Kyiv, paying tribute to his older brother.

Year 1026 to agree to a peace deal under which Russian lands were divided along the Dnieper River, right bank of Kyiv land owned by Jaroslav, and the left bank of Chernihiv - Mstislav. This procedure reign lasted 10 years - to the death of Mstislav.

So only from 1036 Jaroslav edinovlastnym became ruler of Kievan Rus.

Development of Kyiv

About the time of Kyiv Yaroslav the Wise said that he was an ornament of Russia and rival of Constantinople.

Area "of the city of Yaroslav" compared to the stronghold by Vladimir increased sevenfold. Another higher and stronger walls and steel shaft.

Highlight of Kyiv were Golden Gate - the main entrance to the city - Gate Church of the Annunciation, St. Sophia Cathedral, the church of St. George, built in honor of the patron saint of Kievan prince [church name Yaroslav - George (Yuri)] and the Church of St. Irene.

The real gem of ancient architecture, the greatest building in Kyiv time of Yaroslav the Wise was the Hagia Sophia.

It was great, topped the 13 stone church domes, surrounded on three sides by two rows of open galleries.

From the west to the cathedral was added two towers with wide spiral staircase to the church choirs.

Even better than the external grandeur, pryholomshuvalo interior of the church. Most impressed wall image - frescoes and mosaics, among which many were dedicated to the founder of the church - Prince Yaroslav the Wise.

Kiev Sofia symbolizes the combination of the divine and of the government. The cathedral was once a major, metropolitan church of and place of the most solemn state ceremonies - Climbing the Grand table, acceptance of foreign ambassadors and others.

That document researchers call "true Yaroslav" or oldest true.

It consisted of 18 articles, which launched the famous collection of laws - "n the truth."

The articles dealt Jaroslav
