4. Formation of Galicia-Volyn principality

Consequently, the year 1199 began a new chapter not only in the lives of Volyn prince - in memory of posterity Autocrat of all Russia, but also in the history of Ukrainian lands. For those researchers indicate the year of birth of the Galicia-Volyn principality.

The capital of the united principalities Roman chose Galich, considering it the most important task to overcome the resistance Galician nobility.

By Roman Mstyslavych plans were also extend the authority for the remainder of Ukrainian lands.

Year 1202 he had a fight with the prince of Kiev Rurik Rostislavich, as a result of which the latter denied Kiev table.

Not intending himself to reign in Kiev, Roman sat there, under the assumption of some researchers, a close Igor Yaroslav, according to others - a governor.

However, the real ruler of Kiev and Pereyaslav principality subordinate was Galician-Volyn prince.

Tu his power confirmed by numerous campaigns against the Polovtsian organized for the protection of Pereyaslav and the Kiev region.

Among the reasons are called Roman to Galich, researchers have called not only uncertainty Prince of Galician nobles.

Many weighed for Roman Mstyslavych participation in European events. So he took an active part in the internecine struggle Polish princes, and in 1205 was involved, as suggested by historians, in opposition to the German Hohenstaufen dynasty and the House of Welf.

With its commitment to Hohenstaufen, Galicia-Volyn holder out march through Poland to Saxony.

However, his former allies prevented advancement - Polish princes Lesco White and Conrad. In the battle of the Vistula Zawichost on June 19, 1205 Roman died.

Create Galicia-Volyn principality headed by the "autocrat of all Russia" Roman the Great was an important milestone in the history of Ukrainian state, as opposed to multi-ethnic Kievan state of Galicia-Volyn Ukrainian based on a single basis. This gave grounds Ukrainian historians call the novel "the creator of the first national Ukrainian state," that as a separate political body lasted until the late 14th century.
