Demographic status of Ukrainian lands in the late XIX - early XX century.

In Ukraine, as in most of Europe, inhabited by representatives of different nations and religions. Almost three quarters of all Ukrainian population were significantly dominating the other groups in eight of the nine provinces Dnieper Ukraine. But next to them lived a lot of Russians, Poles, Jews, Germans and people of other nations.

Non-Ukrainian population was distributed unevenly. In general, cities in Dnieper Ukraine nineteenth century. rapidly, were centers of non-Ukrainian culture. The same situation was at Western.

Regardless of the size and geography of residence, some of these people were living apart from their own laws, schools, customs and culture, which sometimes differ significantly and was completely alien neighbors Ukrainian. However, these people lived in the Dnieper near the Ukrainian, considered it their homeland and were single community.

Russians and Poles, although they were a minority in Ukraine, belonged to the leading sectors of the political, social and economic life. Jews, Germans, Tatars and other peoples, usually conducted separate economic and cultural life. For Jews defined "boundary settlement", east of which they were forbidden to settle.
