He wrote poems in Latin, Polish, Ukrainian, historical writings, sermons, philosophical and political treatises, books and more. FEATURES OF CULTURE

The development of Ukrainian culture in the second half of XVII - the first half of the XVIII century. occurred in rather complex, sometimes contradictory conditions.

National War in the middle of the XVII century. and revival of the Ukrainian state has contributed to the rise of the creative spirit of the Ukrainian people.

An important factor in the development of the spiritual life of Ukraine was the cultural politics Ukrainian hetman, aims to support the Ukrainian Orthodox Church, National Education, typography and art.

However, the development of Ukrainian culture spryamuvavsya dismemberment Ukrainian lands and unequal conditions of their development.

The desire to establish control over the Ukrainian cultural life - a characteristic feature of the colonial policy of any of the States owned land in Ukraine. For instance, the offensive of tsarism to the statehood of Ukraine took place simultaneously with the increase in pressure in the field of culture.

Heavy blow to Ukrainian culture was the subordination of Ukrainian Orthodox Church (1687).

Another step of the tsarist government, which increased the tsarist intervention in political life of Ukraine were all sorts of censorship harassment Ukrainian typography. In particular, since the 20's. XVIII century. implemented strict censorship control of the Synod of Kyiv and Chernihiv printers. It was forbidden to print books in Ukrainian.

After the Battle of Poltava Ukrainian affairs education came under strict control of the imperial government, by imperial decree had reduced the number of students of Kyiv-Mohyla Academy (two thousand pupils left in 1710 only 165 persons); removed from teaching people who, according to Russian military commanders, were not loyal to the king.

In addition, the tsarist government encouraged the outflow figures of Ukrainian culture in Moscow.

By Peter And so zmanyuvannya elite became violent features: high positions acquired only from Ukrainian leaders who favored the king, and in all obeyed his will.

The highest ecclesiastical dignitaries of the empire were Ukrainian (Stefan Jaworski, Theophanes Prokopovych).

There was a time when all the episcopal chair covered in Ukrainian.

It was the most tragic in the fact that for high positions Ukrainian had to deny their roots.

Do not avoid harassment in the fields of culture and Western. For example, most colleges on the Right Bank and western Ukraine was controlled by the Jesuits.

Ukrainian remained just the beginning of communal and public schools, but they persecuted the Polish magnates.

EDUCATION AND BOOK. Kyiv-Mohyla Academy

The lowest level of education were primary schools. Their numbers during the 18th century. growing steadily.

The first half of the 18th century. on the lands of the Left Bank Hetman and Slobozhanshchina operated colleges - Chernigov (1700), Kharkiv (1726) and Pereyaslav (1738).

In those times Ukrainian colleges were vsestanovymy: they learned not only the children of the priests, Cossack and nobles, but also people from peasants and townspeople. Number of Students in Colleges was significant: for example, in Chernihiv trained annually 700-800 people.

Higher education institutions in Ukraine, the main focus of science and art during the second half of XVII - the first half of the XVIII century. remained Kyiv-Mohyla Academy.

Grateful memory of the founder entrenched in the name of the board that during his lifetime became known as Metropolitan of Kyiv-Mohyla.

As for the status of the Academy, it is perceived as newly created contemporaries. Although a formal terms Academy was recognized in 1658 in Hadiach contract.

Support Hetman Ivan Mazepa promoted flowering of this institution, to 1709 it studied up to 2 thousand. Students from different regions of Ukraine, as well as Belarus, Russia, Moldova, the South countries.

BAROQUE ARCHITECTURE OF UKRAINIAN

Ukrainian architecture of the second half of XVII - XVIII centuries. defined as Baroque architecture.

The word "baroque" in Italian means bizarre, strange. So-called artistic style that prevailed in Europe from the late XVI and almost to the end of XVIII century.

For Baroque art is characterized by extreme attention to form. In the literature, this feature appeared in a variety of genres - until such verbal exercises like curly poems and pyshnomovnosti: in verbose build complex sentences and applying intricate unexpected comparisons, the accumulation of images, combined with the ancient biblical mythology and more. In the XVII century. Baroque was even writing letters (indicative in this respect is the signature Ukrainian hetman and clerks). Baroque structures inherent accumulation luxurious decorations, accented by decorative, grandeur. Ukrainian lands Baroque style was most clearly in the work of architects Y.Shedelya, S.Kovnira, I.Hryhorovycha Barsky, B.Rastrelli, B.Meretyna, painters Y.Kondzelevycha, I.Rutkovycha, sculptor I.Pinzelya; in the music of Baroque art is represented by names such as M.Berezovsky, D.Bortnyansky, A.Vedel in the literature - I.Velychkovskyy, H.Hrab'yanka, S.Velychko.
