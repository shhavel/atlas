The leading position of Germany in Europe manifested even clearer after the unification of its western and eastern lands. As a member of the European Union and NATO, the Federal Republic of Germany is a bridge between East and West, between Scandinavia and the Mediterranean countries.

Germany - the fourth (after the US, China and Japan) country in the world in terms of GDP and a leader in exports. In the European Union it is the leader in all respects. Germany - a highly developed post-industrial state, the main sectors of the economy which are services and industry. In an industry dominated by engineering (especially automotive, ship-, aircraft, machine tools), electric power, chemical industry. Well-known worldwide brands Mersedes, Audi, BMW, Porsche, Siemens, Adidas, Bayer has long become symbols of impeccable German quality and reliability.

The country has a highly intensive modern agriculture, which is 3/4 satisfies the needs of the population in food. The central position of Germany in Europe resulted in a high proportion (over 40%) of transit in handling all types of transport. Transport corridors Germany coupled with the countries of Central and Eastern Europe. For a project to create a single European transport network provides the formation of corridors 9 - polimagistraley - parallel roads of one or more modes of transport, which communicate between the same areas and transport hubs.

Economic and geographical situation of the countries of Central Europe, is very convenient. In the global economy, they are states of transition economy with an average level of development. In the early 90-x's. States in the region (except Belarus) took the path of radical reforms in all spheres of public life. Significant progress towards a market economy reached Slovakia, Poland, Lithuania. They belong to the industrial-agrarian country. Industry is developing on their own resources and imported energy. Prominent place in the industry of mechanical engineering belongs (the production process equipment, machine tools, automotive and precision engineering). Czech Republic belongs to the industrial countries (the share of industry in the national income accounts for 60%). Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland, Lithuania is a member of NATO and the EU.

Belarus is also a country of transition, but it is characterized by a more modest rates of economic development. The reason for this gap in industrial relations with former Soviet countries, and also in the complex transformation processes that occur in this state. During Soviet times, Belarus was a kind of assembly shop, working on a broad cooperative relationships with companies of almost all the republics of the USSR and the Comecon countries. So now Belarus in its external economic relations focuses primarily on Russia and neighboring countries, speaking for the strengthening of integration processes in the CIS.
