In the modern world the state can not exist in isolation. Between them there is a versatile communication and relationships (economic, military, financial, human, etc.). In order to regulate interstate relations established international organizations. Today in the world there are thousands of them. According to the purpose of international organizations are divided into general political (UN, NAM, EU, CIS) and special created for specific tasks:

military (NATO, ANZUS);

economic (ASEAN, WTO, OPEC, NAFTA);

financial (IBRD, IMF);

intellectual and analytical (Club of Rome).

Some international organizations are global, others - regional.

The largest and most influential international organization is the UN - a global general political organization established in 1945 with the aim of maintaining and strengthening peace and international security and cooperation between the countries.

The principal organs of the United Nations: General Assembly, Security Council, Economic and Social Council (EKOSOR), the International Court. Specialized UN agencies on specific issues, UNESCO, FAO, WHO.

Regional organizations are formed by groups of countries in certain regions of the world, united for a variety of applications within the community of interest.
