General Information

It lies in the far west in the basin of the upper reaches of the Western Bug and the Dniester. The surface of the southern mountain region of the Dividing Range (g peak, 1408 m), Verhnodnistrovskymy (750 m) and Skole Beskids (g Parashka, 1268 m). North of the mountain pass in the height of the Carpathian Mountains, in the valley of terraced Dniester, and on Hill Ridge - in the bore (397 m). Next - Holohory (g Kamula, 471 m) and Voronyak (440 m) pass in the lowlands surfaces of small Polese and Verhnosanskoyi plains. In the extreme north-east - Volyn Upland. The region is rich in natural resources - oil, natural gas (gas-Pre-Carpathian region), coal (Lviv-Volyn coal basin) and peat. There are deposits of potash and rock salt, sulfur, mineral springs and mud. Historical Background

The first human settlements in the region referred to as the late Paleolithic. Archaeological finds near the village. Vysotsky (Brody district) gave the name "Vysotsky culture." In 1199 Lviv region (Galicia) was attached to the Volyn Prince Roman the Great. The prince and his son Daniel consolidated Galicia-Volyn principality was founded many new cities. It was then founded the city of Lviv, named after the son of Galician Prince Daniel Romanovich. After the collapse of the Galician Principality of land annexed to Poland (1349), and in 1772 they went to Austria. After the collapse of Austria-Hungary in Eastern Galicia was proclaimed West Ukrainian National Republic (1918). Soon Lviv was occupied Poland. During the interwar period Lviv land became one of the centers of the national liberation struggle of the Ukrainian people. During the Second World War there formed part of the Ukrainian Insurgent Army, which fought for the idea of ​​independence of Ukraine. In Ukraine gained independence in 1991 the Lviv region has played a leading role.
