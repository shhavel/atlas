General Information

It lies in the northeastern part of Ukraine. Surface - undulating plain, with a slight slope to the south, dissected by river valleys, gullies and ravines. There are snowdrifts (1-5 m tall) on the terraces of the Seversky Donets and salt dome structure. The northern part of the region - spurs Serednoruskoyi hill. From west to south Poltava plain changes in the Dnieper Lowland, and further to the southeast - in Donetsk height. The area is rich in fuel resources: natural gas and oil (oil and gas Dnieper-Donetsk region), coal. Is rock salt, phosphates, ocher, non-metallic materials for metallurgy (refractory clay, molding sand) and raw materials for building materials (clay, sand, chalk, limestone, unique sand for the manufacture of optical glass, etc.). There are mineral springs. The climate is continental with relatively mild winters (-6,6 째, -8 째 in January) and warm dry summers (+ 19,5 째, + 21,5 째 in July). Precipitation is 495-570 mm per year. Rivers length of 10 km - 156 and the main tributaries of the Seversky Donets (Uda, Mozh, Bereka (right), Wolf, Big Burluk, Oskol (left)). Samara River, a tributary of Orel and Vorskla (Merlot, Kolomak) belong to the basin of the Dnieper. In the region of 50 reservoirs, about 1 960 ponds and lakes of the channel Dnieper-Donets Basin. Among the black soil types typical medium and ordinary black, deep low- and middle. On the right bank of the Seversky Donets - light gray under oak forest and gray forest soils. In the north-west - dark gray ashed on Borovoy terraces sod-podzolic and sod sand; on river terraces - saline soils and salt in flood - meadow and marsh soils. In the forest-steppe (northern) part of the region characterized by mixed forests (linden, oak, maple, ash) and meadow steppes; in the steppe zone - fescue-feather grass steppes with gorge forests on the slopes of hills. There are pine, and oak-pine forests and floodplain areas with wetland vegetation. The fauna includes 330 species, including mammals - 61 species of amphibians - 11 birds - 211. Adverse natural processes, intense plane wash on steep slopes - landslides, wind and water erosion is linear; salinity, waterlogging and siltation of water bodies. Reclamation: flow regulation, erosion control and agroforestry activities. In the region 191 territories and objects protected areas, including national importance: 3 sanctuaries, botanical garden, zoological park, arboretum, 4 park monuments of landscape architecture, 2 regional landscape parks, six natural reserves .

Historical Background

Kharkiv earth is on the verge of the forest-steppe and steppe zones. This has left a mark on all the early history of the region. At the time of Herodotus lived here Scythian farmers. In the Middle Ages, in the valley of the Seversky Donets dominated by nomads. Agricultural population, including Slavs, originally started to explore the land, were forced to retreat to the north. Only in certain periods of the land in distributed power Pereyaslav, Chernigov and Kiev princes. At the beginning of the XVI century. the territory of modern Kharkiv region was the deserted spaces. Slowly she falls under the control of the Moscow tsars, the power which represented the governor of Belgorod. Depopulated lands began settling the Ukrainian peasants and Cossacks. In the 1638-1639 biennium. Chuhuiv they founded and Raisin in the 1655-1656 biennium. - Kharkiv, and by the end of the XVII century. - The rest of the major cities of Kharkiv. Land was called Slobozhanshchina. Cossack regiments encountered here were subject to Belgorod governor. In 1765 the regimental system was abolished, while established Sloboda-Ukrainian province, which was later renamed to Kharkiv. At the end of the XIX century. Kharkiv is one of Ukraine's largest cities - more than Kyiv. With the victory of the Bolsheviks in the civil war in Ukraine in 1920 Kharkiv became the capital of the Ukrainian SSR and it is up to 1934 Today Kharkiv - one of the most important industrial and cultural centers of Ukraine.
