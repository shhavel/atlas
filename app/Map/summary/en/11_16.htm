The most dramatic events in the political history of Ukraine's independence day unfolded during the presidential elections of 2004-2005. United democratic forces put forward a presidential candidate Viktor Yushchenko. Pro-government structure, supported by Leonid Kuchma nominated a single candidate in the person of Viktor Yanukovych - the current Prime Minister and former head of State Administration, Donetsk region. Yanukovych went to the polls under the banner of the Russian language official status, the introduction of dual Russian-Ukrainian citizenship and was based on the full support of the state apparatus Ukraine, as well as sympathy and assistance to the President of the Russian Federation. Yushchenko is in his campaign emphasized the Ukrainian patriotism and democratic values. Extremely brutal election campaign before the elections described the political poles of Ukraine, Donbass, who stood by his countryman Yanukovych and Galicia, who wanted to see Ukrainian President Yushchenko actually. After the elections, held on November 21, 2004, the Central Election Commission declared Yanukovych the winner (he won 49.46% of the vote against 46.61% of the vote for Yushchenko). But during the elections was detected as abuse, fraud and flagrant disregard for the rights of voters who outraged the public hit the streets of Ukrainian cities to protest against tyranny. Centre confrontation became Kyiv Independence Square, where for several weeks continued a rally in support of Viktor Yushchenko. These days is known as the Orange Revolution. Eventually the authorities were forced to make concessions and agree to another round of elections, held on December 26, 2004 Viktor Yushchenko supported the central and western regions, Viktor Yanukovych - South and East. Under the watchful eye of public and international observers, electoral fraud were negated. As a result, Yushchenko became President of Ukraine, gaining 52% of votes.
