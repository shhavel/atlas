The development of Ukrainian culture in the late nineteenth - early twentieth century.

Ukrainian culture of the second half of the XIX century., In spite of all obstacles, continued to flourish. This was due to: first, the reforms of 1860-1870 years (abolition of serfdom, the judiciary, regional, municipal, educational reform); Secondly, the rapid development of capitalist relations.

Changes in education have already felt on the verge of 1860. Young intellectuals, students, united in the community actively engaged in the establishment of Sunday schools. In 1862 in Ukraine there were more than 110 teaching many of them were built in the Ukrainian language were published primers and textbooks, including "Primer" Shevchenko. But that same 1862, the tsarist government decided to close Sunday school, and many of the organizers and teachers were arrested.

Policy reforms carried out simultaneously with the policy of the tsarist government repression of Ukrainian culture. This is known and testified Valuev Circular 1863, which hampered the development of Ukrainian culture. Only since the beginning of 1870 in Ukraine revived cultural and educational activities. At this time, significant support school education to become zemstvo. They increase the appropriation for the maintenance and construction of schools. From 1871 through 1895 appropriations increased by 6 times. New blow to the development of Ukrainian culture caused Ems decree of Alexander II in 1876 but then do not disappear progressive trends in culture. In 1865 on the initiative of Pirogov Odessa founded Novorossiysk University. Thus, the Ukrainian lands were already five universities. Established as Nijinsky History and Philology Institute of Technology Kharkov, Kyiv Polytechnic, Ekaterynoslavsky Mining Institute.

Some achievements in the field of education was achieved in Western Ukraine, opened a number of universities, including the University of Chernivtsi (1875), Lviv Polytechnic Institute and Academy of Veterinary Medicine from 1869 zaprovadzhuvalos compulsory education of children aged 6 to 14 years.

A new phenomenon was the emergence of public research organizations. At the Kiev University research community: philological, mathematical, physical, medical, obstetric and gynecology, Society of Nature Historical Society of Nestor the Chronicler. In Western Ukraine in 1873 was created Literary Society. Shevchenko, which in 1892 transformed into Scientific Society. Shevchenko (Shevchenko Scientific Society). Socially, there are three leading scientific sections - historical and philosophical, philological and mathematical-drug, and a number of committees, including archeographical, bibliography, ethnographic, legal, statistical and others. In 1880 is flourishing Ukrainian theater. This was due to the fact that only in the theater was possible to develop the Ukrainian language. The best playwrights were also directors and managers theater company. Ukrainian professional theater came at the amateur groups operating in the 1860-1870 years. In 1882 under the leadership of M.Kropivnitsky Elizabethgrad created the first Ukrainian professional troupe, to which were invited M. Sadowski, M. Zankovetska, A. Markov, I. Burlaka et al. In 1883 the troupe was headed Starytsky, and remained director M. Kropivnitskiy. In Western Ukraine Ukrainian professional theater was founded in 1864 O. Bachynskyi called "Russian conversation."

In the musical art distinguished by creativity SS Gulak-Artemowski. In 1862 he created the first Ukrainian opera "Cossack beyond the Danube."

The founder of Ukrainian classical music was MV Lysenko, who wrote in this period of great opera "Christmas Night", "drowned", "Nedorosl", "Taras Bulba" operetta "Chernomorets", an opera for children, "Mr. Kock" "Goat-dereza 'and so on. In Western Ukraine should mention the work of M. Verbitsky.

Thus, the second half of the XIX century. was difficult and controversial period in the development of Ukrainian culture. Despite the difficulties, the culture was enriched by remarkable achievements in all major industries.

Cheremshyna), which in their work seamlessly combine traditional etnohrafizm national literature, the story in the first person and others. with the latest European achievements - and psyhoanalizmom symbolism.

It should be stressed that the Ukrainian version of modernism was quite original and has its own characteristics. Due to the fact that the Ukrainian lands had their own state, had been separated and were actually in the status of provincial social development, they have slow compared with the leading European countries, hence the conflict between civilization and culture, the artist and society were not as sharp . These factors are identified and obscure, poorly expressed, immature nature of Ukrainian modernism. Some World-ups only vidtinyaly general provincialism and deep traditional Ukrainian culture.

Ukrainian modernism emerged as a national not original current, and was detected only in the works of different artists. This style, especially in literature, was a significant influence of romanticism, because of how tradition and mentality of the Ukrainian people, to which romanticism is an organic element in the outlook any day. The peculiarity of the Ukrainian version of modernism lies in the fact that he was of the aesthetic phenomenon into a cultural and historical phenomenon, was an attempt to overcome provincialism, of secondary importance, secondary Ukrainian national culture, a form of involvement of mankind. He seemed to symbolize the transition of Ukrainian society from ethnographic consumer identity, ie distinguishing themselves from others, to national self - determination of their place and role in the modern world.
