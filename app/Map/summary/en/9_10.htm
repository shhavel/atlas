Ukrainian culture first half of the nineteenth century.

National revival in the first half of the nineteenth century. occurred mainly in the field of culture.

Despite the low educational level of the population, continues to develop education. Educational institutions were four categories: 1) parochial schools - for people of the lowest states; 2) county schools - for the nobility, merchants, government officials, artisans; 3) school - for nobles and officials; 4) high schools and universities - for the nobility. The majority of Ukraine's population was illiterate. While Ukraine has 19 schools, 2 high schools - Odessa (1817) and Nijinsky (1832), Kiev Theological Academy and 2 universities - Kharkov (1805) and Kyiv (1834). In Western Ukraine were: Lviv University (1661), Lviv Technical Academy (1844), Kremenetskiy Lyceum (1805).

During the growth of national consciousness in Ukraine since seeing renewed interest in national history. Ukrainian intellectuals began to explore their own history.

Famous Scientists - Notes on Nikolai - a prominent Ukrainian historian, social activist (1817-1885 gg.). Written by well-known work on the history of Ukraine: "Bogdan Khmelnitsky", "Ruin"; the history of Russia - "Russian History in zhyzneopysanyyah Her hlavneyshyh deyateley." Notes on was a professor at the University of Kiev. Nikolai known as a writer and romantic. By following collections: "Ukrainian Ballads" and "Vitka". Posted historical drama "Sava Chaly" and "Pereyaslav night."

Nicholas A. Markevitch (1804-1860 gg.) - Ukrainian historian, writer and ethnographer. Published book "Ukrainian melodies." Known for his historical work in 5 volumes of "The History of Little Russia." Its very interesting collection of documents on the history of Ukraine seventeenth-century HVIII.

Michael A. Maksimovic (1804-1873) - a prominent Ukrainian historian, naturalist, linguist, folklorist and ethnographer. Wrote an interesting historical drama "came from a story by Russian ground Nesterova stories and other writings of ancient Rus'. He also is the author of a number of works on the history haidamak movement, has published many valuable documents on the history of Ukraine.

MO Maksimovic was also a renowned botanist, one of the earliest precursors of the evolutionary theory of the English scientist Charles Darwin.

Kharkov University in the first half of the nineteenth century. became the center of scientific research. A well-known professor of mathematics T.F.Osypovskyy was rector of the Kharkov University in 1813 to 1820 he created the three-volume "Course of Mathematics."

Michael Ostrogradsky - outstanding Ukrainian mathematician who founded St. Petersburg mathematical school. His major works cover the issues of mathematical analysis, mathematics, physics, analytical mechanics. Mikhail was a close friend of Taras Shevchenko.

In 1817 Lviv was created by cultural and educational institutions called "Lviv Institute Ossolinskis" (Ossolineum). The founder was M.Ossolinskyy - Polish educator, bibliographer, literary critic and historian. The Institute has two divisions: the library and museum. Institute Ossolinskis was educational center at that time.

Ukrainian literature of the first half of the nineteenth century. represents bright titanic figure of Taras Shevchenko (1814-1861 gg.). Creation of the Ukrainian genius had a huge impact on the spiritual development and national self-consciousness of the Ukrainian people. His major works, published in 1840 "Kobzar" 1841 - "Hajdamachy" 1843 - "Nazar Stodolya" 1845 - "And the dead and the living" and others. were not only a literary phenomenon, but also a political manifesto. Taras was a great Ukrainian poet, artist, thinker and public figure.

It is worth mentioning and other creators of modern Ukrainian literature, especially Kotlyarevskyi (1769-1838 gg.). In 1798 he published the poem "Aeneid." Important role in the development of drama played play "Nedorosl." In addition to literary activity Kotliarevsky took part in public life in Ukraine.

P.Hulak-Artemovskij (1790-1865 gg.) Has written several dozen poems and fables. The famous tale of his "Lord and Dog", published in 1818

Kulish (1819-1897 gg.) Creatively worked on the development of Ukrainian culture and literature, the awakening of national consciousness and patriotic Ukrainian. Kulish trying to develop ideological program for the Ukrainian people, dreaming of state building in Ukraine, insisted on the idea of ​​equal unity Ukraine and Russia. Famous historical works Kulish: a three-volume "History of Russia vossoedynenyya" brochure "Ruthenians and Poles Easter eggs at Easter 1882" poem "Kulish in hell" novel "Black Board".

G. Flower-Osnovyanenko (1778-1843 gg.) - A prominent Ukrainian writer. Most known for his satirical works, "Gentry Elections", "Shelmenko Batman," "Courtship in Goncharovka", "Konotopska witch."
