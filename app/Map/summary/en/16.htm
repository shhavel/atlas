General Information

It lies on the Dnieper Lowland. The surface is flat and generally bowed to the southwest. The most elevated north-eastern part of the region - Poltava plain (to 202 m) wide valleys left tributary of the Dnieper. In Mesopotamia there uplift due to salt structures (Vysachkivskyy hump), common passage steppe valleys and saucer. The region is rich in mineral resources, of which the most important are deposits of natural gas and oil (Dnieper-Donetsk oil-bearing region) and iron ore (Kremenchug iron ore district). Revealed deposits of granite and granodiorite to the south; throughout - clay and sand, sometimes peat. There are sources of mineral waters (Myrgorod, United Bagachka, New Sanzhary). The climate is continental with moderately cold winters (-6,5 째, -7,5 째 in January) and warm summers (20 째, + 21 째 in July). Here there are dry winds in summer, in winter - ice and freezing ground. The precipitation - 450-565 mm per year, mostly in summer. In the 62 river flowing length of 10 km, belonging to the basin of the Dnieper. The largest of them - Sula with Orzhitsa and Udayem, Psyol of Hkorol and Hovtvoyu, Vorskla. In the region around 1225 ponds. Soils mainly - black typical and normal (70% of the area). In the south - saline soils and meadow-chernozem hlybokosolontsyuvati in combination with saline. A large area occupied by forest gray and ashed and peat-bog soils. The natural steppe vegetation is almost preserved. Forests and forest belts occupy 7.5% of the area planted in the banks of rivers, ravines and on sand dunes. The fauna is represented by 400 species, including mammals - 56 birds - over 300 species of amphibians - 11 reptiles - 11. Adverse natural processes: linear and planar erosion, landslides and occasionally Shishaki; floodplains and wetlands passable valley and wetlands; is suffusion-sagging phenomenon; contamination of soils and water bodies in the field of exploration and production of oil and gas. Many silver and gold items era of early tribes found in Novosandzharskyi area. In X-XIII c., At the time of Kievan Rus, Poltava was part of Pereyaslav principality. Here, on the steppe frontier, facing the fortified city - Lubni, Pyriatyn, Horol and others to protect against attacks Polovtsian. Then based and the current administrative center - the city of Poltava, which is mentioned in chronicles in 1174 after the Mongol invasion in the XIV century. Poltava region came under the rule of Lithuania, and later - Poland (XVI cent.). Unit middle of XVII century. Poltava land - the core of Ukrainian Cossack state. It formed Cossack regiments Poltava, Mirgorodsky, Hadiach, Lubensky. In the 1708-1709 biennium. Poltava land was the scene of race war between Sweden and Russia. In 1709 Poltava was defeated troops of Charles XII and Hetman Ivan Mazepa. This event ended the autonomy of the Ukrainian Cossack state. At the end of the XVIII century. Poltava is part of Chernigov province, in 1802 created the province of Poltava, which included 10 counties. During the national liberation struggle and civil war 1917-1920., During the famine of 1932-1933. And during the Second World War 1941-1945. Poltava land has undergone severe tests.
