The African continent has been and remains the least developed region of the world, called mainland backward, agrarian and raw material periphery of the world. All the states of the continent, except South Africa, belong to developing countries. For most of them characterized by economic and political instability, the narrow specialization of the economy, the low level of overall development, poverty and low life expectancy. 33 of the 47 least developed countries are in Africa. However, some countries of the continent due to the export of raw materials have managed to achieve a fairly high rate of GNP. Thus, in 90% of Libya's foreign trade income yield extraction and processing of oil; in Algeria are the leading oil and natural gas, phosphate rock and metal ores. Egypt's major exports are cotton and oil. For the production of cotton country ranks first in Africa, and for the production of high-quality long-staple cotton - first in the world. In other countries of the region is dominated by agriculture, which employs 60 to 80% of the working population. However, due to low productivity and the extensive nature of agriculture provides only 2/3 of local needs for food. In some countries, the development of export plantation areas with monocultural specialization. On exports of cocoa beans are allocated to Ghana, Ivory Coast and Nigeria, peanuts - Senegal, Niger and Gambia, palm oil - Nigeria, coffee - Ethiopia, Kenya, Uganda, vanilla - Madagascar, dates - Kenya, Tanzania.

Livestock production in the region has extensive character. The most developed animal husbandry (breeding buffaloes, mules, zebu), breeding of camels and sheep, whose products (except fur and leather) used for own needs of the population. Most of the animals used as draft power.

Africa is conventionally divided into two subregions: North Africa and tropical Africa (south of the Sahara, and to South Africa), or into five regions: North, West, East, South Africa and Equatorial.

Saharan Africa - the most backward part of a group of developing countries. This is the least urbanized region in the world. North Africa - the more developed regions due to geographical location and availability of mineral resources.
