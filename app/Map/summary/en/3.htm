General Information

It lies in the north-western part of Ukraine. The general slope of the plain surface from the south (292 m) to the north (139 m). The northern part - Volhynia Polesie with mixed forests, swamps; south of it - Volyn hilly ridge. The southern part is occupied Volyn Upland, which is dissected by ravines and river valleys. The mineral resources are the most important deposits of Lviv-Volyn coal basin. There are peat, limestone, chalk and chalk marl, Lesia, sand, mud and mineral springs. However, the availability of mineral resources is insufficient. The climate is continental with mild winters (in January -4,4 °, -5,1 °) and warm humid summers (July + 18,8 °). Precipitation is 550-640 mm per year. The region main European watershed dividing the Black Sea and Baltic Sea (in accordance with the Dnieper and the Western Bug). Total rivers (length of 10 km) 130. The largest river - Pripyat and its tributaries Turia, Stokhid, Stir. 24 rivers flow into the Western Bug River (the largest of them - Grasslands). The region - 22 lakes with a total area of ​​14.4 thousand. Ha. Group of lakes of karst origin - Shatsky Lakes, including the deepest lake in Ukraine Svitjaz. Typical floodplain lakes are white and Lyub'yaz. The region - 384 bids, 10 reservoirs and 3 475 artesian wells. Soils - sod-podzolic (35.8% of the area), fertile humus-carbonate in the south - turf and meadow (18%), dark gray and black ashed in river floodplains - meadow and marsh, peat. In Polesie common pine forests, birch groves, rarely spruce forests south - mixed forests of oak, beech and pine. The fauna is very diverse (301 species of animals). Among the adverse natural processes observed high spring tide, flooding rain, atmospheric drought, dust storms, flooding and waterlogging (north more than 60% of the territory), karst processes in the watersheds of tributaries Pripyat, in the Volyn Upland - linear and planar erosion sufoziyno- prosadochni phenomenon. Reclamation measures: drainage of wetlands, erosion control afforestation, reforestation, reclamation of waste land. In the 374 territories and objects protected areas, including national importance: the national park, 16 nature reserves, natural monuments 3, Botanical Garden, 3 parks, monuments of landscape architecture, regional landscape park, 27 natural reserves .

Historical Background

The name of the Volyn received from the same old town, which lay about twenty kilometers from the date of Vladimir-Volyn. From VII to X in. these lands were inhabited by Eastern Slavic tribes: Dulebs Buzhans and Volinians. In X - XII century. Volyn was part of Kievan Rus as Lutsk independent principality. Since the second half of the XII century. These lands included in the Vladimir-Volyn, and since 1199 - to the Galicia-Volyn principality. Vladimir-Volyn was one of the centers of Galicia-Volyn principality, which included the territories of modern Western Belarus and Western Ukraine and Moldova. In the middle of the XIV - XV century. Volhynia was ruled by the Lithuanian princes. Lublin Union of 1569 united Poland and the Grand Duchy of Lithuania into one state - the Commonwealth, and Volhynia was annexed by the Kingdom of Poland. In 1793 Volhynia part of the Russian Empire as a dependent. By royal decree in 1797 was created instead Volyn province. In July 1919 the oblast's territory was occupied Poland. Riga agreement in 1921 legitimized the domination of Poland in this region. It was established Volyn province. Since March 1939 Volyn incorporated into the Soviet Ukraine. During World War II, Volyn forests are a refuge fighters against invaders. On the historical past of the region today resemble architectural monuments Volyn cities that have preserved medieval Western flavor, combined with the legacy of Kievan Rus and Cossack Ukraine.
