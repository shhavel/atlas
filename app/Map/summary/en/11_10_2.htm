Religious Life in Ukraine at the beginning of.

Ukrainian people has long been espoused two forms of Christianity - Orthodoxy (from the tenth century.) And Greek Catholicism (from the XVI century.). After years of the Ukrainian Greek Catholic Church regained its legitimate activities (1989). The vast majority of its adherents live in western Ukraine. Greek Catholic Metropolitan or "Archbishop of Lviv and all Ukrainian" Pope approves.

Ukrainian Orthodox divided into several churches. Most parishes have Ukrainian Orthodox Church-Moscow Patriarchate (UOC-MP), which is derived from the former Ukrainian Exarchate of the Russian Orthodox Church. Its main center is located in Moscow, Ukraine, the UOC-MP is the "Metropolitan of Kyiv and All Ukraine". In 1992 began operating independently of Moscow, the Ukrainian Orthodox Church-Kyivan Patriarchate (UOC-KP). Most of the faithful living on the Right Bank and western Ukraine. Ukrainian Autocephalous Orthodox Church (UAOC) follows the tradition of Ukrainian Orthodoxy to its absorption Russian Orthodox Church in the late XVII century. Unsuccessful attempts were made to restore the Ukrainian autocephalous in 1920-1930, 1941-1944. It was only in 1990 Ukrainian Autocephalous Orthodox Church was able to officially resume his activities. Its main centers are also located in the right bank and western regions.

Another form of orthodoxy is the Old Believers. His supporters distanced themselves from the official church in the XVII century. In Ukraine there are several Old Believer communities. Traditionally, this doctrine followed by Russians.

To the Roman Catholic Church are mainly Poles, Hungarians living in Ukraine. However, there are many and Ukrainian Catholics. The greatest influence Catholicism has Zhitomir, Vinnitsa, Khmelnitsky and Ternopil regions.

Protestant doctrine began to spread in Ukraine XVII cent., Mass in the nineteenth century. The most influential Protestant churches are the Union of Evangelical Christians-Baptists of Ukraine. Other common Protestant Pentecostalism is that represented by several organizations, Adventism (Seventh Day Adventist Church) and reformatstva. Protestant denominations include representatives of all social groups and all nationalities in many towns and villages of Ukraine.

Some profess faith in Ukraine only members of certain nationalities. Yes, Lutheranism respected the Germans and Latvians. Believers Armenians belong to the Armenian Apostolic Church. Judaism is the national religion of the Jews.

Ukrainian Muslims living in the Crimea and the cities east and south. Islam is practiced Crimean Tatars, Tatars, Azeris, the representatives of other nations, for whom Islam is a traditional religion.

In the last decade in Ukraine there are adherents relatively new to this area faiths, Jehovah's Witnesses, Mormons, Buddhists, Hare Krishna, bahayitiv, daosystiv, ushanuvalnykiv revival of pagan cults.
