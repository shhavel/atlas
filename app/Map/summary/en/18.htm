General Information

It lies in the northeastern part of Ukraine. The surface area has a general slope to the southwest. The eastern part of the region - on the spur of Serednoruskoyi Hills, West - in the Dnieper lowland (105-120 m). The central part of the region is the accumulative-denudation plain dissected by ravines and gullies. Most industrial importance Fossil fuels: oil (one third of stocks Ukraine) and natural gas, peat (floodplains of major rivers in the north). There are non-metallic minerals, phosphates, rock salt, potassium salt, limestone, marl, mineral paint, plaster, chalk, refractory and refractory clay, construction and glass sand. The climate is continental with relatively cold winters and warm summers. Precipitation - 510-588 mm per year, 60% of falls in the warm season. Territory of the region occurring 165 rivers longer than 10 km, belonging to the basin of the Dnieper. The largest of them, the Seimas of the gums, with Vorskla Vorsklytseyu, Sula tributaries Romain and Turn, Psyol of serum Horol. Within the region there are many lakes and swamps, reservoirs and 26 1 188 ponds. The soils are predominantly black (53%), gray forest and sod-podzolic - in the north, in the flood year - meadow, meadow marsh and swamp. 17.6% of the area is covered with forests, located mainly in the north. The main species - oak, pine, birch, aspen, alder and ash. The fauna includes about 355 species, including mammals - 60 birds - 238 fish - 40 species of amphibians - 10. In adverse natural processes in common: water erosion, suffusion-sagging phenomenon, formation of gullies and landslides, karst ( where the chalk and marl), waterlogging (in floodplains and bottoms of ravines), salinity, deflation (on sandy river terraces). The main goals of improving soil fertility - establishment of protective plantations. Historical Background

At the end of the III millennium BC. E. e. the lands of Sumshchyna lived various tribes of farmers and ranchers. Archaeological finds the time referred to Maryanivka culture. In the VIII century. e. e. there lived Scythian farmers, and in VII. - Slavic tribes northerners. In the IX century. Sumy was part of Kievan Rus, was one of Pereyaslav and the Chernigov principality, and the XII century. - To Novgorod-Seversky. At the time it was built many villages and cities. But the fierce civil wars weakened the principality, which led to frequent attacks Polovtsian (the "Lay" the events of those times when Prince Igor Svyatoslavovych was defeated by nomads and taken prisoner). From the Mongol invasion in 1239 affected the ancient cities in the area, many of them were destroyed completely. Much of the territory of Sumy region in the XIV century. captured by the Lithuanians. According to a truce in 1503, which ended the war with Lithuania in Moscow, almost all of Chernigov and siverian lands ceded to Muscovy. During the XVII century. land of Sumy region were the subject of war between Russia and Poland. Here were the line between Ukrainian Cossack state and Slobozhanshchina - wide strip of land that they have mastered the Ukrainian. In 1658 the new lands were created Sumy and Ahtyrsky regiments subordinated Belgorod governor. In the XVIII century. the city was the seat of Deaf Ukrainian hetman to the abolition of the Hetmanate in 1764 in the XIX century. land Sumshchyna divided between Chernihiv, Poltava, Kharkov and Kursk provinces in Soviet times - between the same area.
