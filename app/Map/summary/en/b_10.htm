Migration - Latin - relocation. External Migration - a transfer of population from one country to another with the change of the place of residence permanently or for a long period. In external migration distinguish emigration - leaving their country to another and immihratsiyu- enter the country the citizens of another state. On the number of population affected by external migration, including immigration contribute to the mechanical growth of population. The difference between the number of emigrants and immigrants is called migration balance. It can be positive (if the prevalence of people arrived) and negative if the number of those who left the country more than the number who came to her.

External migration occurred ever. They played an important role in human development, particularly in national structure of the population of many countries (US, Canada, Australia, South America, etc.). Causes external migration may be economic, political, religious, family and household, environmental, natural (associated with natural phenomena - earthquakes, droughts, etc.). Economic factors are the main cause and the basic number of migrants worldwide. For economic reasons distinguish labor migration (job search) and brain drain (search-paid intellectual work of scientists and people of creative professions).

An important problem associated with external migration is the problem of refugees. About 20 million people have become in recent years forced migrants - refugees as a result of international and regional conflicts, civil wars, acts of hardship, hunger and poverty. Each country anyway faces challenges of migration, making migration policy. Depending on the demographics and demands of their economies, national governments stimulate or restrict the arrival of foreigners.
