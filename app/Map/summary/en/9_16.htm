Resettlement and Ukrainian emigration in the late nineteenth - early twentieth century.

The process of consolidation of capitalist relations in Ukraine was accompanied by a shortage of arable land and landless peasantry complete. Farmers lost their main source of livelihood, sought a return to the traditional way of life. Thus, the first and main goal of Ukrainian peasants are finding free land.

Continued launched since the end of XVIII - early XIX centuries. Ukrainian migration movement in the Volga and the North Caucasus. In the second half of the XIX century. number of Ukrainian population reached almost 400 thousand. people. in the Lower Volga and 1.3 million. Caucasus. Over 100 thousand. Ukrainian were in Kazakhstan and Central Asia. Ukrainian settlers brought their economic experience to the field of new settlements, introduced in the tillage practice is still not known culture buckwheat, corn, sugar beets, tomatoes, strawberries and more.

Ukrainian emigrated in sparsely populated while the regions of Siberia. At the end of the XIX century. them there were about 225 thousand. people. Ukrainians have made a significant contribution to the development of the Far Eastern expanse and other outlying lands of the Russian Empire: the unusual climatic conditions, hard work drained swamps, forests uprooted, settled new lands.

Boosting Ukrainian movement resettlement to the East gave the Stolypin agrarian reform 1906 - 1916 рр.

From Galicia and Transcarpathia Ukrainian mainly emigrated to the USA, Canada and Brazil. Interested in cheap labor for agriculture and industry, governments of these countries in 70-90-ies of the XIX century. encouraged mass migration from abroad. The bulk of immigrants from Ukraine remitted back to work. In 1877 the first group of Ukrainian emigrant settlers arrived from the forests to the United States, added to the bench miners of Pennsylvania. But most Ukrainian peasants sought to obtain for agricultural cultivation of land - homsted (allotment that was granted funds from public land on favorable terms in the United States and Canada to those who wanted to keep the farm).

Many of them were among the miners, in which earnings battered hardest. Meager salaries received unskilled workers Ukrainian. Despite the physical hardships Ukrainian immigration United States, which at the end of the XIX century. there were over 200 thousand. pers., kept Ukrainian national customs and traditions, realizing a united national community.

At the end of the XIX century. slightly less than the number of was a Ukrainian immigrant communities in Canada. The vast majority were Galician and Ukrainian Bukovina. They settled in deserted places in Western Canada, where he received the Homestead. Having their own farm neighbors in Homestead built their dwellings, formed a settlement with a church, school, post. Thus the Ukrainian village. As Ukrainian-first settlers in the United States, their countrymen in Canada, one of their earliest settlements also called Ukraine.

In newly built settlements Ukrainian immigrants retain national traditions - and especially housing, and arts and crafts (furniture, dishes, clothing). Just celebrating weddings, christenings, funerals, celebrated national and religious holidays, do not forget folk songs brought from the homeland, and create new ones.
