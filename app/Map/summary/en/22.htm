General Information

It lies in the western part of Ukraine on the left bank of the Dniester and Southern Bug and Roztochchia tributaries Pripyat. Surface - enhanced polohohvylyasta Lesova plain. The northern part of the region - low-lying alluvial plain, outwash sands of the loess residuals in the form of hills, which lies within the Polesie lowlands (220-240 m). In the northwest the low ledge separates Volyn Upland. The central part of the region lies on the spur of the Dnieper Plateau (320-380 m). Plateau-like surface Transnistria deeply dissected canyon river valleys, ravines, gullies. South-western part of the region is crossed Tovtry (409 m). Here there are karst landforms and landslides. The mineral wealth of the region - non-metallic minerals, granite, kaolin, limestone, brick and tile clay, loam, sand, chalk, marl, sandstone, gypsum, bentonite clay. There are deposits of rock phosphate and peat, mineral waters. The climate is continental with mild winters (-5 °, -6 ° in January) and warm humid summers (+ 18,3 °, + 19,5 ° in July). Precipitation - 600-640 mm (in the northwest), 500-530 mm (in the southeast) per year. In the region - 165 rivers longer than 10 km. The rivers belong to the basins of the Dnieper (Gorin, happened, Homora, Korchik), Southern Bug (Buzhok, wolf, Zgar, Reeve, Ikva), Dniester (Zbruch canyon, Ternava, Studenica, Ushytsya). Lakes is small (in the pool Horyn). Erected 1858 ponds and reservoirs. Soils - mostly ashed and the typical black soil humus (55.5%), dark gray ashed and gray forest. In the north - sod-podzol. Natural vegetation covers 20% of the territory, of which 6% - meadow steppes and meadows, 1% - peat. Area of ​​forest - about 279.3 thousand. Ha (hornbeam, oak, linden, ash, in the north - pine, spruce, birch). The fauna is represented by 311 species: mammals - 60 birds - 190 species of amphibians - 11 reptiles - 10 fish - 40.

Adverse natural processes - intense plane washout. Eroded soils occupy 30.9% of the agricultural land. On steep slopes of hills and river valleys - landslides. In Transnistria in Mesopotamia - waterlogged soils. In areas where the carbonate rock - karst processes. Reclamation: Revegetation of disturbed lands, clearing rivers, fixing riverbanks, erosion plantations. Historical Background

The first settlements in the region appeared in the Paleolithic, one of the earliest - near the village. Luka Vrublovetska. On settlement antskyh tribal farmers (II-IV century). According to various findings, including tilling calendar shown in the ritual vase. During the VIII-IX centuries. in Khmelnytsky tribes lived Tivertsy and Volyn. In 1199 the land went to the Galicia-Volyn principality. It was built many cities and fortresses that protected the population against attacks of the Pechenegs and Polovtsian. In the XIV century. land became part of the Duchy of Lithuania. Later (early XV.) They took the Poles, it was created Podolsk province centered in Kamenetz-Podolsk. Lands swept Khmelnytsky National War led by Bohdan Khmelnytsky, but for Andrusiv agreement in 1667, they again went to Poland. After combining the Right Bank and Left Bank Ukraine in 1795 was formed Podolia. With a history linked skirts a famous peasant leader Ustym Karmaluk.
