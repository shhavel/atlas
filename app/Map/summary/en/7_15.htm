1. The causes disintegration of Kievan Rus

Historians explain the causes disintegration of Kievan Rus'.

Call between them

  • of large size of the state,
  • diverse ethnic composition,
  • knyazivski usobytsi,
  • absence sustainable manner stolonasliduvannya,
  • Prairie kochovykiv attacks,
  • the decline of the trade route "from the Varangians to the Greeks".

The main reason for most researchers consider the development of feudal tenure, strengthening its patrimonial forms.

Princes were not interested in big power of the Grand Duke of Kiev. Even more - they wanted for themselves the same authority.

Wonder in the 12th century. title "Grand Duke" along with Kyiv had Chernigov, Vladimir and some other princes.

Holders of autonomous principalities to guide their own internal politics, at its discretion, decide questions of war and peace, concluded agreements with neighbors.

Such autonomous principalities in the middle of the 12th century. was about 15, of which 5 were formed on Ukrainian territory, Kiev, Chernigov, Pereyaslavskoe, Volyn and Galician.

Comparison of crown land borders with territories tribal groupings East Slavic tribes that evolved during the settlement period (5-7 in.) Led scientists to believe that many weighed in the disintegration of Kievan Rus different ethnic composition of its territory.

Slowed down for a strong centralized government processes maturing three Slavic nations - Ukrainian, Belarusian and Russian revived as soon as the power and waned and when favorable conditions in economic life.

On the soundness of this view is indicated by the fact that one of the first longer obey the Kyiv principality of Polotsk (Belarus), Novgorod and Vladimir-Suzdal (Russian territory).

Yet, despite the irreversible collapse and growing autonomy lands, principalities, Kievan Rus until the mid-13th century. was the only country - and the only area with common laws and only church.

Kyiv still remained, although increasingly formal, capital city, and for the right poknyazhyty it competed Rus' princes of the various principalities.

As during Monomakh, princes assembled at the Congress, where he addressed the controversial issues - mostly related to the organization of joint campaigns against the Polovtsian.

Each princes, barely won Kyiv table, tried to use his position to strengthen the authority of the Grand and restoration of centralized state.

Modern historians interpret fragmentation is not a collapse of the state, as a change in its structure and form of government. The disintegration of Kievan Rus researchers call a federal monarchy. Form of government characteristic of Kievan Rus era of fragmentation, defined as the collective suzerainty, instead of one power is exercised by the Grand Duke of association influential princes.

4. Pereyaslavskoe principality

Territory

Pereyaslavskoe principality formed by Yaroslav the Wise.

Its territory compared to other kingdoms was small.

To the east and south Pereyaslavshchyny land directly bordering the steppes.

Such geographical location largely determines the life Pereyaslavtsi, because their land served as a shield for the rest of Kyiv Rus and territories.

That is why in Pereyaslav principality measures Grand Prince of Kiev powerful defensive fortifications were constructed.

Mostly as a military fortress and the city appeared Pereyaslav.

Impregnable stronghold was, in particular, and Pereyaslav.

The city is located near the Dnieper River, where the river flows into the Alta Trubizh, and almost as reliable reinforcement that Kuman, which often vdyralys in Pereyaslavskaya land, the city and could not take it.

Features of political life

Immediate proximity to Kyiv and feature predetermined position Pereyaslav princes volodaryuyuchy Pereyaslav, as if they were expecting to have a good opportunity to gain Kyiv table.

Moving some of the princes from distant lands to Pereyaslav meant that a new powerful contender for Kyiv.

This fact explains why Pereyaslavskoe principality generally had no political independence and almost entirely dependent on Kyiv.

As for the princes of Pereyaslav, the prominent figures among them were many. Recall at least Vladimir Monomakh, who reigned in Pereyaslav 20 years.

During the rise of Pereyaslav principality fragmentation is associated in particular with Prince Vladimir Glebovich.

He was a constant participant of numerous antypolovetskyh campaigns, earned fame brave warrior and a gifted military leader.

As Novgorod-Seversky Prince Igor, Vladimir Glebovich immortalized by the author of "Words ...", but not for marnoslavnist and political nerozvazhlyvist, and as an organizer of Defense Pereyaslav.
