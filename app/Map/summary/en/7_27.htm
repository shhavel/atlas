Main presentation

1. Terms of culture

Ukrainian Culture 15th - early 16th century. - The direct successor to the culture of Kievan Rus' and Galicia-Volyn principality.

Ukrainian lands were part of several states, because the conditions for cultural progress Ukrainian prevailing in those states are not the same.

Favourable conditions were in the Grand Duchy of Lithuania. Collecting Ukrainian-Russian lands Lithuanian princes sought to join the great culture of Kievan Rus, whose property is not rejected, and supported and revived with the Orthodox faith and Ruthenian language that were deemed public.

Lithuanian rulers took over and the traditional Russia-Ukraine lifestyle.

On the Polish Kingdom, the researchers say that it was the gate through which came the idea to Ukraine Renaissance, acquired a spread of Western European education system.

Not having its own high school, Ukrainian studied in European universities, collaborating there to Western scientific and artistic ideas, enriching their native culture.

Ukrainians who were educated in Western Europe, emphasized its national identity, adding to their names the word "Rusyn", "roksolanyn", "rutenets", that is Ukrainian.

However, it should be remembered that the wave of Western cultural influences swallow ancestral traditions.

New and old in Ukrainian culture of peaceful coexistence, layering each other.

Consequently stood unique cultural phenomenon.

4. Education

Traditionally education gained in churches and monasteries.

Specially trained clerks, who were called grammar or scribes teach children writing Church Slavonic, elementary arithmetic, prayers and singing.

The existence of a network of primary schools in Ukraine is confirmed by the fact that many Ukrainian enrolled in European universities from the beginning of their existence. And to become a student had to have a foundation of knowledge.

8. Visual Arts

By the mid-16th century. developed monumental painting - murals.

Many frescoes Ukrainian masters preserved in the monuments located in Poland (paintings in the chapel of the Holy Trinity in Lublin in 1418 and the chapel of St. Cross Wawel Castle in Cracow in 1470)
