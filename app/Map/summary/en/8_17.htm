FIGHT Hetman Petro Doroshenko ASSOCIATION OF UKRAINE

Hetmanship Peter Doroshenko on the Right Bank (1665-1676) began under extremely difficult circumstances. Cossack Ukraine was divided into two Hetman. Particularly appalling devastation suffered Right Bank, where as a result of war and civil war had destroyed 65-70% of the population.

Doroshenko first resorted to measures aimed at improving the internal situation.

Strenuous encouraged settlement devastated southern right bank. Consistently defended the interests of the Cossack.

To get rid of dependence Hetman of officers sought to introduce a lifetime hetman's power.

He created a standing army, which numbered about 20 thousand. Serdyuks.

Great spiritual support to the hetman coming to Chygyryn Orthodox Metropolitan of Kiev Joseph Nelyubovych-Tukals'kyy.

The main purpose of our own Peter Doroshenko saw in consolidating his power on the Right Bank and the gradual unification of all Ukrainian lands into one state.

This led to a war between the Commonwealth and the Ukraine.

In February 1666 Council officers supported the political agenda advanced by Doroshenko. It was agreed:

  • expel Poles from Ukraine;
  • an alliance with the Crimean khan;
  • speak on the Left Bank to merge it with the right bank under the authority of a hetman - Doroshenko.

In December 1666 together with detachments Doroshenko Tatars destroyed the 6000th unit under Polish Braylov Bratslavshchina. However, a successful performance does not put an end to discord in Ukraine, but only suppressed its divisions.

The news of the signing of Muscovy and Poland Andrusovo contract painfully struck right-bank hetman and urged him to resume hostilities against Poland.

DOMESTIC AND FOREIGN POLICY JOHN Samoylovych

16-17 June 1672 in Cossack Dibrova between Konotop and held Putivl General Military Council. There, with the consent of the Moscow Prince Gregory was elected hetman Romodanovskoho General Judge John Samoilovych.

In elections Hetman, on board the Cossack Hetman Dibrova negotiated and the Moscow government, concluding an agreement known as Konotop articles.

10 points of the new agreements complement Glukhivs'ka articles and compared them with the more limited the hetman.

For Konotopska sexes

  • hetman prohibited without the king's decree and the officer council to send mission to other states, as well as maintain relationships with Doroshenko;
  • Hetman was not allowed to deprive officers positions, punished without the consent of the officer or board military sentence;
  • Cossack envoys were not allowed to participate in negotiations with representatives of the Polish government in Moscow for about Ukraine.

Characterized 15 years of Hetman Ivan Samoilovych was his desire to create a state with strong aristocratic Hetman's power.

Way to pursue his goal he ubachav to limit the rights of the Cossack rank and file to intervene in state affairs.

It Samoylovych stopped convening the General Military Council and all public affairs with the council decided.

Aristocracy favored the formation initiated Samoylovych bunchukove Company.

Samoylovych defended the officers' interests, helped expand the petty landholding.

Despite the attempts of officers and Konotop items for maintaining order in the Hetman he kept hired (and convivial serdyutske) army, and later formed a few bands willing.

Like some of his predecessors, sought to secure the succession of the Hetman's power. Getman was concerned about his sons, who gave the most influential posts in the Hetman government and large estates.

Bakhchisarai peace treaty

War of the 70's. Between Muscovy, on the one hand, and Turkey and the Crimean Khanate - the other, for the possession of land-bank Ukraine culminated in the signing Bakhchisarai 13 January 1681 peace treaty between the two countries.

Under this contract:

  • Turkey joins South Kyiv region, Bratslavshchina and Skirts;
  • Muscovy joins Left Bank Ukraine and Kyiv;
  • The border between Turkey and Muscovy was placed on the Dnieper;
  • For 20 years, the territory between the Bug and Dnieper was to remain uninhabited.

SIGNATURE "Eternal Peace"

Bakhchisaray Peace Treaty between Muscovy and Turkey was not lasting because they do not meet the interests of the Commonwealth.

Taking advantage of the defeat of a massive Turkish military forces of the Holy League of Vienna in 1683, Poland regained its dominance over most of the Right-Bank Ukraine.

May 6, 1686 in Moscow between Poland and Muscovy, signed a new contract - "eternal peace".

Poland recognized by Muscovy Left Bank Ukraine, Kyiv, Kharkiv, Chernihiv-Siver land of Chernihiv and Starodub.

In addition, the Polish side abandoning claims to Kyiv, which received compensation.

Treaty of 1686 secured:

for the right to own Muscovy Hetmanate Left Bank;

Kyiv region North in Poland, Volhynia and Galicia;

Bratslavshchina and South Kyivshchyna were unoccupied neutral zone between the two countries;

Skirts remained under Turkish rule, though not for long, because 1699 was also incorporated into Poland.

"Perpetual Peace" complicated situation in Ukraine, weakening the national liberation movement, as affirmed violent division of Ukrainian lands among several states.

Crimean CAMPAIGN 1687 SOLUTION POWER OF JOHN Samoylovych

Enclosing the "eternal peace" with Poland, Muscovy joined the states that fought against Turkey and its vassal - Crimean Khanate.

Commitment to anti-Turkish coalition - Holy League consisting of Austria, Venice and the Commonwealth - as well as their plans, which included the elimination of the Turkish-Tatar possessions in the Black and Azov seas, and future access to the Black Sea, prompted Moscow to war.

In May 1687 the 100,000th Muscovite army went to the Crimea. Hike led by Queen Sofia's favorite, most influential person in Muscovy - Vasily Golitsyn.

On the way to Golitsyn's army joined the army of 50 thousand Ukrainian Cossacks led by Samoylovych.

Although great hopes pinned on this military campaign, but the combined Ukrainian-Muscovite army failed even to reach the Crimea.

  • by the fire in the desert, burned by the Tatars;
  • lack of food, water and feed for the horses.

Golitsyn ordered the army to turn back.

Not recognizing their own mistakes, Golitsyn was looking for the perpetrators of the failed campaign, and therefore easily transferred responsibility for Samoilovych.

After Hetman criticized for improper favorite queen of the campaign, and generally opposed the conclusion of the Moscow Government "perpetual peace" with Poland and the alliance with it against Turkey and the Crimea.
