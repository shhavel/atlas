Annexation of Western Ukraine SSR

August 23, 1939 between the USSR and Germany signed a nonaggression pact and secret protocol of the Molotov-Ribbentrop that defined the zone of influence of the two states in Eastern Europe. In this protocol eastern Polish territory (ie the western region of the former territory of Ukraine) had to be transferred to the USSR.

Soviet-German treaty has decided to hand Hitler aggression in Europe. September 1, 1939 Nazi troops invaded Poland. September 17, when the main forces of the Polish army was defeated, Soviet troops crossed the Polish-Soviet border in Western Ukraine and Western Belarus. Beginning of "liberation campaign" Red Army, and the actual implementation of the secret protocol of the Molotov-Ribbentrop. In fact, the Soviet Union entered World War II on the side of Germany, and involved in the division of the world. Polish troops did not resist.

September 22, the Red Army entered the city. On the same day in Brest-Litovsk after successful completion of the Polish campaign, a joint parade of Soviet and German troops. September 28 Soviet-German military-political alliance, sealed compatible fighting against almost defenseless Poland (before German troops occupied Warsaw), was confirmed by the new contract - about friendship and abroad.

To legitimize the Soviet regime in Western Ukraine October 22, 1939 under the control of the new government held elections to the National Assembly, which adopted a Declaration in late October to join the Western Ukraine into the USSR and its reunification with the USSR. In November 1939 session of the USSR Supreme Soviet and the USSR adopted the relevant legislation.

In the western Ukraine was officially incorporated into the USSR, was created six regions: Lviv, Stanislav, Volyn, Ternopil, Rivne, Drohobyckyj.

Less than a year later was legally formalized and incorporated into the USSR territory of Northern Bukovina and the Danubian lands. In June 1940, given the Soviet-German agreement on the division of spheres of influence, the USSR announced an ultimatum to Romania regarding the transfer of Bessarabia and Bukovina. June 28 southern group of the Red Army under the command of Zhukov was passed. Dnestr and came to these territories. August 2, 1940 by decision VII session of the Supreme Soviet of the Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic was created and Northern Bukovina Chernivtsi region organized and submitted it to the USSR. In the USSR were also included Khotyn, Akkerman and Izmail districts.

In November 1940 between SSR and Moldavian SSR was a new frontier. As a result, Ukraine was alienated from Transnistria with ethnic Ukrainian population.

After turning into the USSR Western Ukraine, Northern Bukovina and Bessarabia three districts of Ukraine's population increased by 8.8 million people and the middle of 1941 was more than 41.6 million people and its territory expanded to 565 thousand. Km2.

With annexation of Western Ukraine to the Soviet Union began the process of Sovietization, ie changes in all spheres of life under produced during the Soviet era designs. In the process of Sovietization there are two phases:

- Until the spring of 1940, when the regime remained at least outwardly democratic and reform welcomed the support of most of the population;

- In the spring of 1940 enhances a socialist content transformation, accompanied by mass repression because of the rejection of these changes the majority of the local population.

Support from individuals met the following measures: elimination of the Polish state apparatus, the confiscation of land large owners, nationalization of trade and industry (the industry in the hands of the Poles and Jews), ukrainization and strengthen the education system (number of Ukrainian schools increased to 6.5 thousand. With simultaneous reduction Polish), Lviv University was renamed in honor of Ivan Franko Ukrainian was the language of instruction, etc.), the development of social welfare, particularly health care.

Dissatisfaction caused: replacement of the Polish Communist officials and local people, sent from the East Ukraine, forced collectivization (in June 1941 the collective farms were combined 15% of households), the pressure on the church (like Greek Catholic and Orthodox) defeat of political parties and public associations, including such revered by the people as "Enlightenment" and mass repression.

Repression began almost immediately after the occupation of the land of the Red Army in Western Ukraine, but first they raised mostly Poles - were deported to the East of the USSR million Poles and shot 15 thousand. Polish officers.

Among Ukrainian before others were repressed activists of political parties. The only organized force resistance of the new government remained UNO.
