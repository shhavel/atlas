Countries and peoples have always differed in the extent of providing the population with material and social benefits. The main criterion for determining the level of economic development of the country has a Gross Domestic (or gross national) product per capita.

The division of countries into groups depending on their level of economic development is called typology of respectively sovereign states which are divided into four types.

Economically developed countries (GNP produce 2/3 of the world, there are about 30).

Developing countries (1/4 of the GNP of the world, about 130 countries).

Countries with economies in transition (to 30 countries in Central and Eastern Europe, Northern and Central Asia).

Countries centrally controlled economy: China, Cuba, North Korea, Vietnam.

Also GNP (GDP) in the allocation of new types of use integral indicator of social development of the so-called Human Development Index (HDI), which takes into account the size of the real per capita income, life expectancy and educational level of the population. The highest rates of HDI are economically developed countries (USA, Japan, Germany, France, Britain, Italy and Canada), other countries of Western Europe, Australia and New Zealand.

A typology of countries in terms of socio-economic development of practical significance. Since the UN to solve the problem of overcoming the backwardness of developing countries, determine the state most in need of financial and humanitarian assistance.

Gross domestic product (GDP) is defined as the aggregate value of all final products produced in the country during the year.

Gross national product (GNP) is composed of GDP for subtracting the profits of foreign companies in the country and with the addition of profits earned by entrepreneurs of the country abroad.
