General Information

It lies on the northwestern Ukraine. The most northern part of the region stretched within the Polesie lowlands (132-200 m), which is a low-lying plains ploskohvylyastu with wide terraced valleys, with a vague marshy watershed. The southern part of the area that lies within the Volyn Upland (200-342 m) - high, undulating Lesova plain dissected by a dense network of rivers on separate boards (Mizotskyy logs) as well as ravines and gorges. In the extreme south-west - Plain Small Polesie. The mineral resources are the most common turf (the largest deposits in Ukraine) - explored about 300 fields, 135 of which are larger than 1 thousand. Historical Background

The territory of Rivne region traces of human presence attributed to ancient times. In the II-III century. The early tribes lived here Zarubynets'ka and Slavic cultures. About 100 remains of ancient settlements. In the X century. These lands were part of the Kievan Rus. Dorohobuzh and Peresopnytsya (now the village) in the XI-XII centuries. were centers of feudal principalities. In XIII-XIV centuries. Rivne region was part of the Galicia-Volyn principality, and the second half of the XIV century. fell under the rule of Lithuania. After the Union of Lublin in 1569 - under Polish rule. In the XVI century. an important political and cultural center of the city was. Ostrog. Near the village. In 1921 Rivne went to Poland as part of Volyn province. In 1939 - annexed by the Soviet Union. During World War II, m. Rivne was the administrative center of the Reich Commissariat "Ukraine".

In years of Ukraine's independence in Rivne revived historical and ethnographic traditions, restored churches, monasteries and ancient castles.
