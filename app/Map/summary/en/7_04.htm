1. The reasons for Greek colonization

Appearance of the first Greek settlements on the Black Sea Historians refer to the middle of 7 cent. e. e.

So the Greek colonies on the Black Sea - is, in fact, a city of immigrants Cypriots in free, pristine lands. To relocate to a foreign country of the Greeks led to various reasons:

  • desire for enrichment through the development of trade: in this new land settlers hoped to lay the city - original commercial basis, through seeking to sell products to distant lands Greek masters;
  • search for sources of raw materials for craft activities Greeks, after Greece - a mountainous country, so the inherent lack of land and limited natural resources and other natural resources;
  • escape from poverty at home.

In the colonization of the Northern Black Sea has played a major role Greek city of Miletus.

Development of the Greek colonies

Every Greek city on the Black Sea coast was built according to plan and in line with the laws of City Planning mainland Greece.

All major cities were surrounded by strong defensive walls with towers and had fortified harbor.

Inside they are divided into quarters.

In the center was located the main square - Agora along with it - the holy place of the temple - TEMENOS.

Around the agora built important public buildings. In some cities they roztashovuvaly a fortified hill - the Acropolis.

Each city had a farm district - choir. Choirs made up of small villages and towns, or, where residents live on gardening and horticulture, growing agricultural products for sprodazh.

Together with a well every town was the separate state-policy. Black Sea city-colonies retained full independence in respect of the Greek cities, migrants from which they were laid.

At the same time ties with the motherland never torn: it is her settlers traded, took part in the cultural life of Greece and others.

Household

Independent existence of the Greek policies of Northern Black Sea was based on the fact that each of them provided himself with everything necessary for life.

The leading occupations of the colonists were growing grain, grapes, vegetables, fishing and fish processing, animal husbandry.

Be noted that the craftwork - metalworking, pottery, weaving, manufacture of glass, wood and bone.

Instead of Greece, imported metal products, steel, textiles, precious jewelry, utensils, spices, olive oil and wine.

4. Bosporus

One of the centers of Greek colonization in the northern Black Sea coast was the Kerch Peninsula.

On both sides of the Kerch Strait, which the Greeks called the Cimmerian Bosporus, 7 c. e. e., there were a lot of policies, such as: Panticapaeum, Nymphs, Myrmekion, Tyritake, Phanagoria more.

Around 480 BC. E. e. there arose Bosporus, which brought together more than 20 Greek cities.

The structure of the Bosporus state also included agricultural areas of the Crimea and Kuban, inhabited by local tribes. The capital of the state was Panticapaeum. Bosporus state ruled by kings.

At the time of highest elevation Bosporus state (4 in. BC. E.) Held its territory of Kerch and Taman peninsulas, Eastern Azov, the lower reaches of the Kuban and the Don delta. In addition to Greek immigrants lived there many local tribes.

Among the reasons that led the Greek city-states to unite, researchers called the first conquest of the militant threat Scythians.

During the heyday of Bosporus was the main supplier of grain to the cities of the Black Sea and Greece. Major trading partners were bosporytyan Athens.

From the second half 2 tbsp. e. e. position Bosporus state began to deteriorate. Increasing threat from the Scythian state in the Crimea.

Uncertain in their abilities, the Bosporan king Perisad V about 107 BC. E. e. king of Pontus handed power Mithridates Eupator VI (Pontus - a kingdom in Asia Minor).

During the negotiations on the transfer of power in the Bosporus rebellion broke Scythians led Savmaka.

The rebels killed Perisad proclaiming their leader king. But a year later, punishing rebels, Mithridates VI Bosporus attached to his possessions.

However, this did not add any strength of Bosporus, or the kingdom of Pontus, which infringe the Roman rulers. Soon Bosporus state fell under the domination of Rome.

Bosporus state lasted until the second half of the 4th century., Having gone through several periods of growth. However, former greatness has been achieved. Losing power into trouble, it eventually fell under the onslaught of the Huns.

5. The decline of the Greek city-states of the Northern Black Sea Coast

History Bosporus clearly indicate that there are two periods in the history of the ancient city-states of the Black Sea.

The first Greek historians call (it lasted from 7 to 1 cent. BC. E.), The second - Roman (1 c. BC. E. 3 c. BC. E.).

Period, depending, as it usually happens in history, was not happy to Greek cities.

Every year, they lost their power and wealth, as suffering from the onset of the barbarians, and from internal strife and discord.

By the 70's. 4. Almost all ancient states of the Northern Black Sea declined. Survived only Chersonese and Pantikapaion, which fell under the power of the Byzantine Empire, were under her protection.
