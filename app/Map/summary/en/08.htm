Slavic peoples took to the path of Christianization and state formation in IX-XI. But at the end of VII century. in the northeastern part of the Balkan peninsula appeared the first Bulgarian kingdom - the state, bringing together local Southern Slavs, Bulgarians and immigrants. During the VIII-IX centuries. the gradual combination of Turkic Bulgars and Slavs into a single nation. Finally, it ended with the adoption of 864 g. Of the population of Bulgaria Byzantine Christianity. During the reign of Simeon the Great (893-927 gg.) Bulgaria has grown almost to the size of the Byzantine Empire. A more permanent war depleted Bulgaria and caused its decline. In 1018 the first Bulgarian kingdom was conquered by Byzantine Empire.

In 818 g. Of the River Morava, where the tribe lived Moravians, there was one of the earliest Slav states - Great Moravia. Rome and Constantinople launched a brutal rivalry, trying to attract Moravians to their faith. Originally Morava baptized by Roman model from the Franks. Later, in 863 g., At the request of Prince Rostislav Byzantine Emperor sent in Moravia fellow preachers Cyril and Methodius. In order to make clear the new faith to the Slavs, they created the first Slavic alphabet (Glagolitic) and translated the Gospel. After the death of their brother disciples German persecution of the clergy left the country. At the end of the IX century. Great Moravia collapsed and after a while a considerable part of its territory captured by Hungarians.
