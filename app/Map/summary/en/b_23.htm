The oil and gas industry is part of the fuel industry in the world. Oil industry includes production, transportation and refining. Average world oil production is presented on the pie chart. The main suppliers of oil to the world market is emerging. Over 40% of production and 60% of exports go to OPEC - Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries, which brings together 12 countries in Asia, Africa and South America. OPEC was created in 1960 to conduct a coherent policy on the oil market in the interests of the countries - participants. A large proportion of mining and oil exports account for as countries that are not members of OPEC (Russia, Norway, UK). The main consumers of oil are developed countries. They concentrated powerful oil company. Therefore, the main aim of the cargo oil of the Middle East to Western Europe, Japan, China and North America. In some developing countries, creates its own refining capacity (Gulf States, Venezuela), and this increased their share of exports of finished petroleum products.

Gas industry provides the production, transportation, storage and processing of natural gas. Transportation of gas within continents is mainly pipelines (80%) and long distance (eg the Middle East to Japan) gas transported in liquefied state special vehicles. This method of transport leads to a noticeable rise in gas prices, because much of it is consumed in the countries that produce gas.

The largest exporters of natural gas are Russia, Canada, Norway, Algeria, Indonesia, Turkmenistan.
