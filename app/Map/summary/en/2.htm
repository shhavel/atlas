During the years of the Revolution, the Consulate and Empire France was at war against a coalition of seven European countries. Of these six accounted for during the reign of Napoleon Bonaparte (1799-1815 gg.), Who in 1804 proclaimed himself emperor of the French. Napoleonic Wars, which continued the war of the French Revolution, have dramatically changed the political map of Europe. Concentrating forces against Austria, Napoleon regained military success, the French captured Vienna in December 1805 at the Battle of Austerlitz defeated the combined Russo-Austrian army. Under the terms of the peace concluded after Austria, Napoleon gave his possessions in Italy and the Balkans, and found him king of Italy.

1806 Napoleon abolished the Holy Roman Empire of the German nation, while the empire became Austria. In South and West German Confederation of the Rhine was established under Napoleon's protectorate. That same year, at the Battle of Yenoyu and Auershtadtom Napoleon defeated Prussia. In Berlin, the emperor signed a decree "continental blockade" of England. In 1807, after the defeat at Eylau and Friedland, the "continental blockade" was forced to join Russia. This was confirmed by the agreement concluded by Napoleon with the Russian Emperor Alexander I in Tilzyti.

1807 Napoleon invaded Spain and a year later - in Portugal. This resulted in the Iberian peninsula national liberation war against the French invaders. It killed many French soldiers, and the loss of Spain and Portugal shaken popularity of Napoleon.

To establish its supremacy in Europe, Napoleon had to defeat Russia. However, his Russian campaign of 1812 ended in complete defeat of the "great army". Established after the sixth anti-French coalition defeated Napoleon and forced him to abdicate. To power in France returned Bourbon dynasty. France lost all the conquered territory and reduced to the borders of 1792 as Napoleon was sent into exile on Elba.

In the spring of 1815, Napoleon left Elba and landed with a thousand guards in the south of France. Dissatisfaction with the restoration of the Bourbons was so great that 20 days Napoleon, who enthusiastically welcomed the return of the French, the triumphant march passed the country went to Paris for a short time restored the empire. "Hundred Days", as they called this period resulted in the final defeat of Napoleon's troops Seventh Coalition June 18, 1815 at the Battle of Waterloo. Napoleon abdicated for the second time and finished his days in exile on St Helena. Thus ended the Napoleonic era, which cost France more than a million lives.

After the collapse of the Napoleonic Empire in Vienna began an international congress, which assembled monarchs, ministers and diplomats of all European countries, except for the Ottoman Empire. Participating countries have made the post-revolutionary and post-war division of Europe. They were able to reach a compromise, to define the boundaries and structure of the state based on the principle of legitimacy - to protect the interests and rights of "legitimate dynasties." The decision reached by the Congress, formed the basis of a new international order, known as the "Vienna System".

In accordance with the decisions of the Congress of Vienna:

  • France returned to the borders of 1792 .;
  • instead liquidated Napoleon Holy Roman Empire of the German Nation was founded German alliance headed by Austria;
  • restored to pre-war system of Italy, resulting Austria gained predominant influence in Italian affairs;
  • To withdrew the bulk of Napoleon created the Duchy of Warsaw, for it was assigned and Finland;
  • Norway, which also belonged to Napoleon's ally - of Denmark, were transferred to Sweden;
  • proclaimed "perpetual neutrality" of Switzerland;
  • Belgium and Holland were united in the Kingdom of the Netherlands, which together with Prussia and Austria had become a counterweight to France.

At the Congress of Vienna also discussed the problem of protecting Europe from the revolutions and wars in the future. To this end, in September 1815 in Paris, Russian, Austrian and Prussian monarchs proclaimed the creation of the "Holy Alliance of monarchs and nations" and promised to defend the "Vienna System" means all - of peace propaganda for military intervention. Over time, they were joined by other European countries. Countries Holy Alliance gathered periodically to Congress to coordinate their actions. Union lasted almost 40 years. According to the decisions suppressed by armed national liberation and revolutionary movements in different countries. However, the move to stop the progress of the state of the Holy Alliance failed.
