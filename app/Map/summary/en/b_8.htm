Poland in XVI - XVIII century.

Poland - the largest of the West Slavic states. XVI century. considered the "golden age" of its history. During this period, in the reign of the last kings of the Jagiellonian dynasty of Sigismund I (1506-1548 biennium) and Sigismund II Augustus (1548-1572 biennium), and Stephen I Bathory (1576-1586 biennium) in the Polish kingdom reigned peace and tolerance. With the assistance of the monarchs of the country spread and flourish ideals of Renaissance humanism.

The population of Poland in XVI - first half of the XVII century. employs about 4 million people, and its territory was 280 thousand sq. m. km. The capital was Krakow. Due to the conclusion of the Lublin Union of 1569 and the accession of Livonia in 1561 its population had increased to 7.5 million people, and the territory now reached 800 thousand sq. M. km. According to the Union of Lublin from the Lithuanian authorities directly in the Kingdom of Poland entered the Ukrainian lands.

In the XVI century. Poland completed the formation of estate monarchy in which participation in public life have been eliminated by representatives of the merchants and the urban bourgeoisie. Increasingly important role in the decision of all cases of public life began to play gentry. A feature of the political development of Poland in XVII. was that in the country there was not an absolute monarchy and the original gentry republic. King, who is elected by the nobility had no right to make decisions without the consent of the Sejm, which has become the main body in the state.

King Stefan Batory I resumed part of its state in the Livonian War and brought it to the finish. Armistice signed on 10 years in the Yam Zapolskoho to Rzeczpospolita moved Livonia.

King Sigismund III Vasa (1587-1632 biennium) decided to intervene in the events that took place in the Moscow State during the Troubles. He provided covert support False Dmitry I. In 1609 began an open Polish intervention in Muscovy. However, hopes of Sigismund IRS consolidate the throne for his son Vladislav failed. In 1618 between Russia and the Commonwealth was concluded Deulino truce.

Politics Polish authorities caused the aggravation of social contradictions in Ukrainian lands. In 1648 the start of the national liberation war of the Ukrainian people against the Commonwealth. Led her Bohdan Khmelnytsky. On the lands liberated from the Polish authorities were created by Ukrainian Cossack state - "Zaporozhian Host."

After the conclusion of the March 1654 Ukrainian-Moscow agreement on the protection of the royal over the course of the war changed Ukraine. Position Rzeczpospolita complicated. Polish-Swedish War 1655-1660 biennium and subsequent Swedish intervention ("Flood") caused the destruction of the economy and the huge loss of population.

In 1672, renewed the war with the Ottoman Empire, which resulted in Rzeczpospolita lost Podolia. Polish-Ottoman War and continued during the reign of John III Sobieski (1674-1696 AD), who was able to regain lost lands previously. However, his attempts to consolidate royal power and the state as a whole failed. Rzeczpospolita is gradually sinking into the abyss of political anarchy, which led to the fact that at the end of the XVIII century. it ceased to exist as an independent state.
