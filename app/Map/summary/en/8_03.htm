DEVELOPMENT OF UKRAINIAN LANGUAGE

During the XVI - first half of XVII century. there were important changes in the development of the Ukrainian language. They related primarily literary language. From the time of Kievan Rus' long tradition of establishing the two literary languages: old Ukrainian literary (in the second half of the XVI century. Próstoyu language it was called) and Slavo-Ruthenian.

Prósta it was close to the spoken language, it is used very widely. She has written a variety of legal and government documents, polemical Oratorical-apostolic writings, insightful poetry, chronicles.

Language Slavic and Ruthenian - Ukrainian version of the Church Slavonic language. Religious books continue written in Old Church Slavonic, which they called Slavo-Ruthenian. And although it is also exposed to vernacular Ukrainian, but the people remained unclear.

Peresopnytsia Gospel

From the second half of the XVI century. began to appear próstoyu language translations of religious texts:

  • Peresopnitske Gospel (1556-1561);
  • Krekhivsky Apostle (60s. XVI centuries).
  • Volyn Gospel (1571) and others.

The first known book of Scripture portrait of contemporary Ukrainian language is Peresopnitske Gospel.

Translation of Greek and Church Slavonic texts of the four Gospels made Peresopnytsia Monastery Archimandrite Fr Gregory. Translation copied son of Archpriest Michael Sanok.

Manuscript on parchment made from the use of black ink and cinnabar. 482 sheets of it framed in oak boards covered with green velvet.

The book is decorated with exquisite paintings, miniatures and illuminations, limbs, initials, many ornaments that have tangible reasons Renaissance.

In sight witnessed phonetic, grammatical, lexical and syntactic features live Ukrainian language XVI.

EDUCATION. STATE school

Traditionally schools operated by churches and monasteries. Specially trained clerics teach children writing Church Slavonic, the basics of arithmetic, prayers and singing. Initially, such schools existed in major cities, then their number has grown: in the second half of the XVI century. they acted in Lviv, Stry, Rivne, Krementsi, err, Vladimir-Volyn, Zhytomyr on.

Deploying the reform movement led to the emergence of Protestant schools. In particular, they were founded in Hoshcha, Belz, Lviv, Berestechko, Khmelnik.

They created their own schools and colleges and the Jesuits. The main goal of educational activities Jesuit schools saw the conversion as many Ukrainian Catholic faith.

Ostrog Academy

New features of education is most clearly embodied in the work of Ostrog cultural and educational center. It was founded in 1576 on the initiative of Prince Vasili-Ostrozky.

The main goal of his own career, he saw the revival of Ukrainian culture on the basis of orthodoxy. To this end no later than 1578 in jail was established school. The ancient Slavic traditions combined here with the domain of contemporary European education.

In Ostrog school thoroughly studied languages ​​- Church Slavonic, Greek and Latin. Taught in it and "seven liberal sciences" - grammar, rhetoric, dialectic, arithmetic, geometry, astronomy and music.

School program, which was carried out training in Ostrog school, provided primary and secondary education with elements of higher education. Actually, as contemporaries called Ostrog school and the school, and Collegium and Academy. It was the first in Ukraine "Slavic-Greek-Latin 'high school in Ukraine and in the East Slavic lands.

The first rector of Ostrog Academy was Gerasim Smotrytsky - a connoisseur of literature, poet and writer. At the invitation Ostrozky he arrived in jail and led the group of scientists who prepared for publication Ostrog Bible. Gerasim Smotrytsky was the chief translator and editor of this publication.

Fraternal school

Enlightening and educational ideas Ostrog Academy were picked up Brotherhood. The need to resist the offensive of Catholic culture had led to communal schools.

The first such school appeared in Lviv in 1586, and soon they began to form throughout the Ukraine. At the beginning of the XVI century. communal schools were about 30. Enroll they were entitled children of all classes. Kept at the expense of school fraternities. Children of poor families and orphans were trained for free.

The main purpose of communal schools - to provide a good education and nurtured a long tradition and commitment to the parent faith. Therefore, much attention is paid to the study of schools and book Slavonic Ukrainian languages. Traditionally studied and Greek. In those times it was difficult to do without Latin - the language of Western science and diplomacy.

Fraternal school, as well as the Ostrog school were Slavic-Greek-Latin. In addition to these languages, the students are learning the already mentioned "seven liberal sciences".

In 1615 appeared Brotherhood School in Kiev. Prior to its establishment have committed scientists, writers, and poets, leaders of the Orthodox Church, which rallied around the Archimandrite of Kiev Pechersk Lavra Elisha Pletenetsky.

The first rector of the Kyiv Brotherhood school was Job Boretskyi.

In the autumn of 1631 in Kiev, there was another school - Lavra. He founded it Archimandrite of Kiev-Pechersk Lavra Peter May. Soon Lavra school was merged with the Kyiv Brotherhood. Consolidated School began in September 1632 was called college.

Kyiv-Mohyla Academy

Kyiv board (soon Kyiv-Mohyla Academy) was a higher education institution.

Complete training lasted 12 years. A total of eight Academy classes, and the number of subjects exceeded the 30 core academic subjects they were still "seven liberal sciences".

In the first four grades Academy studied languages: literary Ukrainian, Church Slavonic, Greek, Latin and Polish.

All higher science at Kyiv-Mohyla Academy taught, as elsewhere in Western Europe, Latin.

However, care in the academy and the proper knowledge of the Ukrainian language: it is scientific activities teaching staff and students contributed to the development of the Ukrainian language.

Kyiv-Mohyla Academy for a long time was the only institution of higher education for all classes Ukraine, Eastern Europe and the Orthodox world.

DEVELOPMENT BOOK

Establishment of permanent book publishing in Ukraine connected with the name of the Moscow printing pioneer Ivan (Fedorov).

Having suffered persecution in Muscovy, it is in the early 70's. XVI century. arrived in the city.

In 1574 Lviv Ivan gave the Apostle and the first Ukrainian printed textbook for teaching reading - "Primer".

In 1581 in jail F. gave a true masterpiece of antiquarian - Ostrog Bible.

By the middle of the XVII century. Ukraine had 25 printing presses in 17 different towns and villages.

First, there were many small private printing presses, but gradually publishing books concentrated in the largest publishing houses - Kiev-Pechersk Lavra, Lviv Brotherhood, Michael Slozky, Jesuit College Lviv.

The largest center of publishing in Ukraine in the first half of the XVII century. was the printing of Kiev-Pechersk Lavra.

It was founded in 1615, Archimandrite Monastery he Pletenetsky.

Lavra printing house published first book in 1616 .; during the second half of the XVII century. it was published 120 titles - primarily liturgical books and primers, dictionaries, calendars, work on the history and more.
