The political map of Europe has undergone significant change in the 90 years of the twentieth century. and continues to change today. Events of recent years have taken place in Europe, not only changed the face of the continent, but also influenced the whole world.

Unification of Germany and the GDR into a single state. The collapse of the Soviet Union and the emergence of the post-Soviet new political entity - the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS), which includes all countries - former Soviet republics except Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania and Georgia. The latter joined the CIS in 1994. The disintegration of Yugoslavia and formation instead of new sovereign states.

In 1993

The division of Czechoslovakia into two independent states - the Czech Republic and Slovakia. The transformation of the European Economic Community into the European Union. Increased integration processes in Europe in order to create economic and political union within a single European space.

In 1995

Accession to the EU of Austria, Sweden and Finland.

In 2002
