Referring to historical times, you can use the map to understand what is due to its geographical position, wealth, land and other natural resources of the territory of Ukraine kept the scene of wars and aggressions object (the Mongol invasion in the XIII century., The struggle between Lithuania, Poland, Turkey and Russia in the XIV - XVIII centuries., between the Soviet Union and Germany in the XX century.). For centuries the Ukrainian lands were part of other states (the Kingdom of Poland and the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, and after 1569 - the Commonwealth, the Ottoman Empire, Muscovy, in 1795 - 1917 - Austria-Hungary and the Russian Empire).

August 24, 1991 - Day of Independence of Ukraine.

Ukraine is a constitutional republic. Head of state - the president. Legislature - Parliament. His national title - the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine. The highest executive body - the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine.

CONSTITUTION OF UKRAINE (extract)

Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine on behalf of the Ukrainian people - citizens of Ukraine of all nationalities, expressing the sovereign will of the people, based on a long history of Ukrainian state and on the basis of the Ukrainian nation, all the Ukrainian people the right to self-determination, providing for the guarantee of human rights and adequate conditions of life caring about strengthening civil harmony on earth Ukraine, striving to develop and strengthen a democratic, social, rule of law, aware of our responsibility before God, his own conscience, past, present and future generations, guided by the Act of Independence of Ukraine of 24 August 1991, approved December 1 1991 popular vote, adopts this Constitution - the Fundamental Law of Ukraine ".

Adopted by the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine on 28 June 1996.

Presidents of Ukraine

Leonid Kravchuk

The first President of independent Ukraine (1991-1994).

The formation of Ukraine as a new independent state associated with the name of the first President Leonid Kravchuk.

In July 1990, he was elected Chairman of the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine. August 24, 1991 the Ukrainian parliament adopted the Act of Independence of Ukraine. This historic document for the people of Ukraine supported the referendum on December 1 of that year. Simultaneously with the referendum held presidential elections. Honor to appear at the head of the newly independent state, citizens have entrusted Leonid Kravchuk.

In 1994 a democratic way Bob handed power Leonid Kuchma. Today, Leonid Kravchuk - Deputies of Ukraine.

Leonid Kuchma

Elected President of Ukraine in July 1994 and re-elected in November 1999.

In October 1994, shortly after being elected President of Ukraine, Leonid Kuchma issued a program of economic reforms, which defined the overall logic of transformation mechanisms to overcome the crisis of the Ukrainian economy.

After re-election as President of Ukraine Leonid Kuchma proposed a strategy for economic and social development for 2000 - 2004 years.

Viktor Yushchenko

Elected President of Ukraine in December 2004.

The election of President Viktor Yushchenko marked the end of post-Soviet phase of development of Ukraine. Ukraine strategic objectives outlined by the new President, is to build a civil society based on democracy, market economy and full integration into Euro-Atlantic (EU, NATO) and international (WTO, etc.) institutions.
