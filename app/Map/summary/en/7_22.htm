1. Location of Kievan Rus' and Galicia-Volyn principality in the history of the Ukrainian people

The roots of our people - in East Slavic tribes who during the 5-7 centuries. actively settled on new land.

The first East Slavic state - Kievan Rus - was a medieval empire.

Along with old Ukrainian - constituent, the dominant people - it lived and others - Slavic and Baltic, Finnish, Turkic peoples.

Ukrainians from closer co-existence in one state with them were able to clearly understand their distinctiveness, dissimilarity to other nations, which ultimately helped to shape the minds of the people, or as they say the researchers, national identity.

The immediate successor of Kievan Rus' was the Galicia-Volyn principality.

Formed in 1199 by Roman Mstislavovich, it served as the backbone of the Ukrainian state in the next century and lasted until 1340

Saved Galicia-Volhynia Ukrainian state traditions were perceived Ukrainian nobility and later the Cossacks, and revived in the Ukrainian Cossack state.

4. The international significance of Kievan Rus' and Galicia-Volyn state

The role of Kievan Rus in that time the world was determined to a large extent its location on major trade routes.

This applies, in particular, on the way "from Varangians to Greeks" that linked the Baltic and Black Sea.

Important also was the overland route that ran from Kyiv to the west, through Krakow and Prague in southern Germany and the city Regensburg on the Danube.

Of particular importance was the trade partnership Rus lands of the Byzantine Empire - the most developed contemporary states.

Considerable political role of in Europe at that time eloquently branched dynastic marriages of Russian princes.

The military might of the Kievan state enabled for centuries successfully defend against attacks of nomadic peoples, including the Pechenegs and Polovtsian.

Kievan Rus was a kind of shield to protect the peoples of Central and Western Europe from the Mongol invasion.

The greatest influence on the culture of Russian lands was Byzantium.

This was facilitated by the establishment of Orthodox Christianity.

Scientists point to the existence in Europe of two cultural areas - Latin and Greek-Slavic (Byzantine-Ruthenian).

Actually, the latter leading center and were Kievan Rus' and Galicia-Volyn state.

Expression of the international authority of Galicia-Volyn principality was the coronation of Prince Daniel Romanovich.

This event confirms European foreign policy orientation of Galicia-Volyn principality.
