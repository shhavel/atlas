England in the XVI century.

In the XVI century. England went on a small territory. Knowledge of English in Ireland were minor, Scotland is also not included in its composition. At the beginning of the XVI century. The population is about 3 million people, and at the end of the century increased to 4 million. But the capital of England, City of London, with a population of about 200 thousand inhabitants. was one of the largest cities in Europe.

XVI century. England - day of the Tudor dynasty. By Tudor in the country occurred, the Reformation, the efforts of the royal power, was conquered by the sea supremacy.

At the beginning of the century in the cities of England lived no more than 10% of the population. Due to the great geographical discoveries England was at the crossroads of trade routes between Europe and America. This resulted in major changes in the economy. Occurs manufacturing. With the advent of factories in England and abroad is growing demand for English wool. In agriculture, the process started over fences, crossing the landowners to the breeding of sheep.

The Reformation began in England during the reign of Henry VIII Tudor (1509-1547 gg.) King supported the Reformation because he was personally interested in the reform of the church. He decided to divorce his first wife (he had married just six times), Catherine of Aragon and marry Anne Boleyn at court beauty. The Pope refused to divorce. Then Henry VIII went to the break with Rome. In 1534 he was accepted "Act of Supremacy", by which the English Church, derived from the supremacy of the Pope, led by King. Henry VIII a divorce freely. So in England, "top", at the insistence of the king, began the Reformation.

The second half of the XVI century. in the history of England called "Elizabethan". Daughter of Anne Boleyn and Henry VIII, Elizabeth (1558-1603 years) completed the formation of the Anglican Church. In her reign finally became a British colony in Ireland.

Relations with neighboring England Scotland were complicated by the rivalry of the two queens, Elizabeth and Mary Stuart. If the right of Elizabeth to the throne were questionable, the Scottish Queen Mary Stuart a Catholic Pope is considered the only legitimate ruler of England. But Elizabeth win this fight and made her rival penalty.

Son of Mary Stuart Scottish King James Elizabeth threatened war for execution of the mother. To avoid war childless Queen of England acknowledged him as his heir. Since 1603 the English throne took the Scottish Stuart dynasty.

In the reign of Elizabeth of England went to war with Spain over penetration in America. 1585 English pirate in the royal service Walter Raleigh established the first English colony in North America, naming it in honor of Queen Virginia (from Eng. Virgin - Virgo). First, the colony was a haven for pirates, hunted Spanish ships.

Illustrious among the then British pirates was Francis Drake (1543-1596 gg.) The cost of production, captured from the Spaniards, so only one of his expeditions 1577-1580 biennium exceeded the English treasury income in two years.

Spanish King Philip II decided to punish the audacious to the Queen of England, which supported the pirates and executed a Catholic Mary Stuart. The battle between the Spanish "Armada" and the English fleet led by Francis Drake, took place in the Strait of Pas-de-Calais in 1588 and ended with the defeat of the Spaniards. Spain from this blow and could not recover. England became one of the first maritime powers of Europe, "mistress of the seas."
