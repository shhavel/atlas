The African continent has been and remains the least developed region of the world, it is called a continent underdevelopment, agrarian and raw periphery of the world. All states of the continent, except South Africa, belong to developing countries. For most of them characterized by economic and political instability, the narrow specialization of the economy, low level of overall development, poverty and low life expectancy. 33 of the 47 least developed countries are in Africa. However, some countries of the continent managed through the export of raw materials to achieve relatively high GNP. Thus, in 90% of Libya's income from foreign trade make the extraction and refining; in Algeria is a major oil and natural gas, phosphates and metal ores. The main export commodity of Egypt are cotton and oil. In the production of cotton top country in Africa, and the production of high-quality long-staple cotton - first in the world. Africa is divided into two subregions: North Africa and tropical Africa (south of the Sahara to South Africa), or into five regions: North, West, East, Equatorial and South Africa.

Tropical Africa - the most backward part of the group of the developing world. This is the least urbanized region in the world. North Africa - a more developed region due to its geographical location and the presence of minerals.
