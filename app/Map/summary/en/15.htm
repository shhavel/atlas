General Information

It lies on the southwestern Ukraine, south and south-east by the Black Sea. The region stretched within the Black Sea Lowland, which gradually decreases to the southeast (from 150-170 m to 10-20 m). In the northwest - spurs Podolsk Upland (elevations to 288 m) - gently wavy flat plains with deep erosional dissection (up to 80-100 m). In the north-east - spurs Dnieper Upland, flowing watersheds which rise to 160-200 m. The south-west - spurs Pivdennomoldovskoyi plains, dissected by river valleys and asymmetric beams. Availability of minerals is insufficient. There are small deposits of brown coal, building materials (sand, clay, limestone, granite, gneiss), salt, graphite. Equally important are Balneotherapy mineral waters, mud and brine of estuaries. Forests - mainly oak (oak, maple, hornbeam, ash, lime). In the swamps of the Danube and Dniester - willow forests of poplar and alder. Historical Background

In Odeschine found the remains of settlements and towns, they lived Cimmerians, Scythians, Sarmatians, Greeks. Later this East Slavic tribes settled - convict and Tivertsy. In the XIII century. Black Sea was captured by the Mongols and connected to the Golden Horde. From the XV century. These lands belonged to Turkey. Under the agreement Iasi 1791 Odessa region was ceded to Russia. The first mention of the Tatar settlement Hadzhibey that existed on the site of present-day Odessa, appeared in 1415 during the Russo-Turkish War of 1787-1791. Black Sea Ukrainian Cossacks captured the fortified Yeni-Dunya and Hadzhibey. And in 1794 there was founded the city, which in 1795 was renamed to Odessa. Since 1797 Odessa was part of Novorossiysk province, and from 1803 - Odessa, Kherson province boroughs. By the end of the nineteenth century. Odessa Kherson outstripped by population and economic potential and in 1921 became a province and then a regional city. In 1954 the Odessa region attached Izmail. Since then, the boundary of the same.
