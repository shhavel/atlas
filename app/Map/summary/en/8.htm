Autonomous Republic of Crimea (ARC) located in southern Ukraine, the Crimean peninsula and occupies the islands of the Gulf Siwash, bordering the Black Sea and Sea of ​​Azov and the Kerch Strait.

Prior to 1917, Crimea was part of Russia Tauride province. In 1918 there was proclaimed a Soviet Socialist Republic Tauris, 1919 - The Crimean Soviet Socialist Republic in 1921 - Crimean Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic within the RSFSR. In 1945 it was transformed into the Crimean Oblast in the Russian Federation, and in 1954 - the area as part of the Ukrainian SSR.

February 12, 1991 the Supreme Soviet of the Ukrainian SSR adopted the Law "On the reconstruction of the Crimean Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic." March 17, 1995 the Verkhovna Rada of Ukraine adopted the Law "On the Autonomous Republic of Crimea."

According to the Constitution of Ukraine, adopted in 1996, the Autonomous Republic of Crimea is an integral part of Ukraine. The Republic has a Constitution; representative body - the parliament, the government - the Council of Ministers.

The area of ​​the Autonomous Republic of Crimea - 26.1 thous. Km2.

Population - 2018.4 thousand. People. (01.01.2003).

Capital - Simferopol.

Administratively, the ARC is divided into 14 districts and 7 of the City Council. On the territory of Crimea - 16 towns, 56 townships and 956 villages.

Located on the peninsula of the city of Sevastopol has a special status, which is determined by the laws of Ukraine.
